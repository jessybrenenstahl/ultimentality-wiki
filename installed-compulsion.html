    Installed-Compulsion (candidate fourth)

    A candidate fourth corruption from the 2026 adversarial run: a pathology installed by inverting or injecting a controller's sign — a presence in the wrong place that is not one of {self, world, medium} over-extended — which the telos-exemption does not foreclose. Part V · CV · treatise-side

    Definition

    Installed-compulsion — also called corruption by addition or sign-flip — is a candidate fourth corruption exhibited by the 2026 adversarial run. It is "a pathology installed by inverting or injecting a controller's sign rather than by absolutizing a single existing term." It satisfies the genus — a presence in the wrong place — but is not one of {self, world, medium} over-extended.

    In depth

    The telos-exemption (the no-fourth argument) defends only against the captured-propagation case: it rules out an over-present aim. Installed-compulsion slips past that defense because it is a presence of a different kind. As the treatise puts it: the telos-exemption "says nothing against an installed inversion that is over-present without being a term-absolutization of the same kind."

    This matters for the count. The three named Theodicytes are each one of the three terms of symbolic participation absolutized — a single existing term inflated. Installed-compulsion is not produced by absolutizing an existing term at all; it is produced by adding an inverted or injected controller sign. So it is a candidate fourth corruption that the telos-exemption cannot exclude — one of the two counter-instances (with the answerable optimizer) that hold the three-count at the carving tier rather than at foundation.

    It belongs in the record precisely so the part does not commit the textual Nephilim of "a silent promotion of three-ness from carving toward foundation."

    Epistemic & alethic marks

    • E: Derived, CV — the count-fixing claim (that three exhausts the corruptions) is contestable by counter-instance, and installed-compulsion is one such counter-instance.
    • A: As a counter-instance, it is exactly the reason the count-fixing application of the telos-exemption "fails to map exhaustiveness" — the closure-at-three does not map, even though the named corruptions do.

    Distinctions & cautions

    • It is a presence in the wrong place (satisfies the genus) — but not a {self, world, medium} term-absolutization, so it is not one of the three named Theodicytes.
    • It is not foreclosed by the telos-exemption, which defends only the captured-propagation case.
    • It is not sterility/absence — it is a positive installed inversion.
    • Its status is candidate fourth, exhibited by the adversarial run; it is the reason the count is held as a contestable carving, not a settled closure.
