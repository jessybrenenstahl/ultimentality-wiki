    Love = (toward, relational gap)

    Toward-coupling that reduces the relational gap — the discrepancy between current separation and desired bond or continuity of coupling. Part II · AC · canonical

    Definition

    Love = (toward, relational gap). It is a predicate binding: the primitive toward bound under the regulated error signal relational gap. Love is toward-coupling that reduces the relational gap — the discrepancy between current separation and desired bond or continuity of coupling. The controller drives that discrepancy toward zero.

    In depth

    Love is not a primitive and not a feeling in the ordinary sense; it is the binding B(toward) = (toward, relational gap). The actuator is the directional operator toward (admits or increases coupling with a signification); the controller is named "Love"; the error signal is the relational gap. The accuracy discipline that governs the layer applies here: extract the regulated error (relational gap), not the surface delivery channel by which love is expressed.

    Love is one of the four cornerstone bindings, stated authority-canonical and verbatim across the canon. As a composite ingredient it enters Submission = Love ⊕ Fear, where Love and Fear are coupled by opposed-gradient contention.

    Epistemic & alethic marks

    • E: Derived, AC — fixed verbatim by the controlling authority; contestable by contesting the authority.
    • A: The binding aspires to map the regulated error actually driving the named emotion; the general method — extract the regulated error, not the surface delivery channel — is the accuracy discipline that keeps the mapping honest.

    Distinctions & cautions

    Love is NOT a primitive. Treating it (or Fear, Apology, Gratitude) as a primitive rather than a binding is the cardinal error and caps any exposition at 72. The binding is regulation toward a setpoint, not preservation of an invariant.
