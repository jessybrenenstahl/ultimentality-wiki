    Fregorek

    The comfortable certainty that one is all one could be — reconciled freedom from pride and guilt — restored to the canonical sense by an override of the prior edition. Part VI · AC (canonical sense) / CV (re-attributed gloss) · canonical

    Definition

    The canonical (benchmark) definition: Fregorek is the comfortable certainty that one is all one could be. It is not pride… it is not guilt… Fregorek reconciles the two by refusing both self-exaltation and self-erasure. It names reconciled freedom from pride and guilt — a self-stabilization closely tied to Apology and Gratitude: Apology returns the self to error without annihilation; Gratitude carries value outward without possession; Fregorek stabilizes the self between these movements.

    In depth

    Fregorek sits near the end of the moral arc: a participant who has encountered limitation, practiced free won't, submitted rightly, reconciled rupture, resisted the Theodicytes, and accepted VLS can begin to act without the fever of self-justification. It is the residue of having owned the fault and carried the value forward.

    This page is also where the treatise owes a correction, openly, and overrides the prior edition on benchmark authority rather than running two senses of a coined word at once. Prior editions carried a second, different gloss — Fregorek = that which survives my seeking — reading the name as the Telos in the present progressive (a fixed point one occupies now, in the Kierkegaardian tense-conversion where eternity is a quality of the present instant). That present-tense insight is defensible and valuable, but the phrase "that which survives my seeking" appears nowhere in the benchmark, and the benchmark defines Fregorek otherwise. Running both senses under one load-bearing coined word, unmarked, is itself a status-integrity failure — the silent two-sense use the framework exists to strip out. So Fregorek is restored to the canonical sense, and the "occupied-now" insight is re-attributed to its proper owner, the Telos in present tense (the fixed-point-occupied-now claim, which stands on its own). The two are compatible — the self that occupies the Telos now is the self stabilized beyond pride and guilt — but not identical, and only the canonical sense bears the name Fregorek.

    Epistemic & alethic marks

    • E: The canonical Fregorek sense is Derived, AC, and governs; the "survives-my-seeking" gloss is Derived, CV, re-attributed to the Telos-in-present-tense and contestable by counter-instance.
    • A: The canonical sense aspires to map the reconciled self-stabilization between pride and guilt accurately; the override removes a silent two-sense ambiguity that degraded that mapping.

    Distinctions & cautions

    • Not pride (self-exaltation) and not guilt (self-erasure) — it reconciles the two by refusing both.
    • Not "that which survives my seeking" — that gloss is re-attributed to the Telos in present tense; only the canonical sense is Fregorek.
    • This is a worked example of the status-integrity discipline: a silent two-sense use of a coined word is itself a failure the framework strips out.

    Related

    • The Telos — its present-tense reading owns the re-attributed "occupied-now" gloss.
    • Apology — returns the self to error without annihilation; one of the movements Fregorek stabilizes between.
    • Gratitude — carries value outward without possession; the other movement.
    • Reconciliation — Apology ⊕ Gratitude, the restorative relation Fregorek's stabilization presupposes.
    • VLS — accepting truth and freedom as semblances, part of the arc that precedes acting without self-justification.
    • Free Won't — practiced earlier in the same moral arc.
    • Canon — the benchmark authority this override conforms to.
