    The Spectre

    The world-relation absolutized under Fear — the self's engagement with the world made the whole of the act, running to one of two poles: it seizes the world, or it seals it out. Part V · AC · canonical

    Definition

    The Spectre is the corruption in which the self's engagement with the world is made the whole of the act. The relation that should carry meaning is governed instead by Fear, and runs to one of two poles. It seizes the world, or it seals the world out. Both are Fear absolutized over the world-relation: one seizes, the other seals.

    In depth

    • Seizes — domination, fear-bound world-shaping that treats others and things as material to be worked, the maker's hand severed from any answer the world might give back.
    • Seals — misanthropic over-refusal, the boundary climbed to a maximum and locked.

    This edition restores the domination pole; a treatment that names only the cellar (the sealing pole) has kept only half the Spectre. The two poles are not two corruptions but the two faces of a single one — Fear absolutized over the world-relation.

    Canon role-coalition: Warden + Sculptor — the access-and-boundary faculty (the Warden) and the world-mutation faculty (the Sculptor) read as a coalition affinity, never a partition. The seed signature at the primitive layer: refusal, overcontrol, boundary-expansion; seize or seal.

    Epistemic & alethic marks

    • E: Derived, AC — the named corruption, its mapping to the world term, and the genus are fixed by the authority; contestable by contesting the authority.
    • A: Aspires to map a real, recurrent, self-maintaining pathology — world-seizure/sealing — one of the framework's central and best-defended pathologies, mapped accurately.

    Distinctions & cautions

    The Spectre is the world term absolutized, distinct from the self (Nephilim) and the medium (Homunculus). Its two poles (seize / seal) must both be carried; naming only the sealing pole halves the corruption. The Warden + Sculptor coalition is a soft affinity, not an identity — five role-captures do not factor into the three Theodicytes (role–corruption affinities).
