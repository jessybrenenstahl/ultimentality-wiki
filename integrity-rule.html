    The Integrity Rule

    Approximation honesty is protective; false closure degrades the system — the direct contrapositive of the capability rule, and what VLS protects. Part I · FT · canonical (seed's sharpest single line)

    Definition

    The integrity rule is stated in the seed's sharpest single line: "Approximation honesty is protective. False closure degrades the system." It is the direct contrapositive of the capability rule. Where the capability rule says higher capability comes from better organization under constraint, the integrity rule says pretending the constraints are gone — false closure — degrades the system that does it.

    In depth

    The integrity rule is what VLS protects. Because all output is constrained approximation, treating that approximation honestly is protective; claiming a closure one does not have is corrosive.

    The error posture operationalizes the rule: when contradiction appears, do not hide it, do not smooth it too early; localize it, revise it, re-express after correction.

    With VLS made active, the integrity rule acquires teeth: silently promoting a claim from a more-contestable tier to a less-contestable one — and above all, promoting any Derived claim into "forced/foundational" — is itself a breaking of the framework. That move is the textual Nephilim: self-certification committed on the page. False closure is the textual Nephilim's effect on the system that commits it.

    Epistemic & alethic marks

    • E: Derived, FT — the contrapositive of the capability rule, contestable only by declining that rule.
    • A: Maps a real, general failure mode of meaning-bearing systems; false closure is the textual Nephilim's effect on the system that commits it.

    Distinctions & cautions

    • It is the contrapositive of the capability rule, not a separate principle: deny the constraints and capability degrades.
    • "False closure" includes the tier-promotion move — quietly upgrading a claim's contestability tier, or stamping a Derived claim "forced." That is the textual Nephilim.
    • The error posture forbids both hiding a contradiction and smoothing it too early; correction comes before re-expression.

    Related

    The Capability Rule — the integrity rule is its direct contrapositive. VLS (Verietliberisimilitude) — what the integrity rule protects. The Textual Nephilim — false closure is its effect; tier-promotion is committing it. Derived (the single epistemic status) — promoting any claim out of Derived breaks the framework.
