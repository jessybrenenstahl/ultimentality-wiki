    Two-Layer Separation

    The corpus keeps two separate failure layers — three system-level Theodicytes (self/world/medium) and five SPLCW role-captures — and five does not factor into three, so no forced partition between them can hold. Part V · AC (the distinction & coalition relation) / CV (the specific edges) · canonical (distinction) + treatise-side (correction)

    Definition

    Two-layer separation is the structural correction that the framework holds two distinct failure registers: Layer A, the three named system-level Theodicytes, each defined by which non-telos term over-dominates at the self/world/medium layer; and Layer B, the five SPLCW role-captures, one failure per role internal to the meaning-processing cycle. These are complementary layers, not a relabeling — and five does not factor into three.

    In depth

    The single most important structural correction in this part is that the version-churn came from wrongly fusing the two layers — trying to force the five role-captures into a clean partition under the three Theodicytes.

    Layer A — three system-level Theodicytes, each by which non-telos term over-dominates, read at the self/world/medium layer: Spectre (world), Nephilim (self), Homunculus (medium).

    Layer B — five SPLCW role-captures, one failure per role: Warden → rigidity; Captive → collapse and confusion; Logician → sterile flattening; Poet → decorative drift; Sculptor → premature action.

    Because there are five roles and three Theodicytes, "five does not factor into three — which is exactly why any forced partition produces a contradiction somewhere." The canon confirms the layers are distinct and that role → Theodicyte is a coalition relation, not an identity (Spectre = Warden + Sculptor; Nephilim = Captive + Logician; Homunculus = Poet), and is explicit that this mapping evolved and was corrected over time — direct evidence that it is a revisable affinity, not a settled derivation. The "Logician/Poet flip" that drove the churn "is entirely an artifact of trying to align Layer B one-to-one under Layer A; grant that the alignment is a soft affinity rather than a partition, and there is nothing to flip."

    Epistemic & alethic marks

    • E: The two-layer distinction and the coalition relation are Derived, AC; the specific edges are Derived, CV soft affinities (contestable by counter-instance).
    • A: The two layers aspire to map two genuinely distinct failure registers, and the non-factoring of five into three is the accurate reason no clean partition exists.

    Distinctions & cautions

    • The layers are complementary, not a relabeling; do not collapse Layer B onto Layer A.
    • Role → Theodicyte is a coalition (soft affinity), never an identity or partition — see Role–Corruption Affinities.
    • The mapping is a revisable affinity, not a settled derivation (the canon corrected it over time).
    • The "Logician/Poet flip" is an artifact of forced one-to-one alignment, dissolved by holding the affinity soft.

    Related

    The Theodicytes — Layer A, the three system-level corruptions. SPLCW — the role cycle whose five captures form Layer B. Role–Corruption Affinities — the soft coalition edges between the layers. The Spectre — world absolutized; coalition Warden + Sculptor. The Nephilim — self absolutized; coalition Captive + Logician. The Homunculus — medium absolutized; coalition Poet. The Logician — the faculty over which the seed and benchmark edges contend. The Poet — paired with the Logician in the "flip" the separation dissolves.
