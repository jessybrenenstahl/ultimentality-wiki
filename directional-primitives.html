    The Directional Primitives

    Four bare directional operators — toward, away, loop-back, propagation — primitive directional structures, not feelings and not metaphors. Part II · AC (operator-status and the four semantics) · canonical

    Definition

    Beneath the affective and moral architecture sits a base layer of bare structure. Its first rung is the four directional primitives: toward, away, loop-back, propagation. They are operators, not feelings — "primitive directional structures, not feelings and not metaphors." The felt qualities of the same names are gloss, carrying no probative weight.

    In depth

    The derivational order the corpus protects runs: directional primitives → predicate bindings → conceptual derivatives → composites → SPLCW operator chain → failure states → applications. This Part treats the first two rungs and the axes that close them. The four primitives sit at the base.

    The seed names the routing pair inward and outward, with symbols → ← ↺ ↻; the rename to loop-back and propagation is exact and structure-preserving — terminology, not architecture. The seed's binding rules pre-empt the cardinal error directly: predicates do not define the primitives; primitives are not reducible to the predicates; these are not to be read as ordinary human feelings.

    The four are not emotions, moods, virtues, images, or personality styles. They are directional operators — bare, unexperienced structure. The set is not merely listed at four; it is carved at four through the Selection and Routing axes (see below). One discipline governs the whole layer: Fear, Love, Apology, and Gratitude are predicate bindings, not primitives.

    Epistemic & alethic marks

    • E: Derived, AC for the operator-status and the four semantics — fixed by the controlling authority; contestable by contesting the authority.
    • A: The four definitions aspire to map the elementary directional moves of any transformation step; that the operators are bare and unexperienced is itself the accurate claim, against which the felt gloss has no probative weight.

    Distinctions & cautions

    The primitives are NOT the emotions named like them, and not metaphors. The predicate does not define the primitive; the primitive is not reducible to the predicate. Collapsing the operators into the bindings is the cardinal error, capping any exposition at 72.

    Related

    Toward — admit / increase coupling, on the Selection axis. Away — exclude / decrease coupling, on the Selection axis. Loop-back — route structure back in, on the Routing axis. Propagation — emit structure outward, on the Routing axis. The Selection Axis — the domain-side axis the primitives are carved by. The Routing Axis — the codomain-side axis the primitives are carved by. Predicate Binding — the next rung: a primitive paired with a regulated error. The Cardinal Error — inverting primitive and binding.
