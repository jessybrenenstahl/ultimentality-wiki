    Continuity

    The fourth operational consequence: because output is approximate, continuity depends on symbolic preservation and reconciliation carrying structure across time. Part I · FT (frame-internal) · canonical

    Definition

    Continuity is the fourth of the axiom's operational consequences. Because output is approximate, continuity depends on symbolic preservation and reconciliation. This binds the axiom to memory and to the affective layer.

    In depth

    Since no participant possesses the case directly and every output is an approximation under constraint, persistence across time cannot rest on perfect, infallible retention. It rests instead on symbolic preservation plus reconciliation — structure carried forward symbolically and corrected as it goes.

    The preservation side binds the axiom to memory: retain meaningful structure, permit revision, reduce drift, and preserve long-range consistency without pretending infallibility. The reconciliation side binds it to the affective layer, where Reconciliation names the inward-correction-plus-outward-return composite that carries structure across time.

    The treatise flags an important structural point about that composite: Reconciliation is a coupled controller, not a sum — it is not simply Apology added to Gratitude. The mechanics of that coupling are developed in Part III; here continuity only names the consequence that such a carrying-across-time is required, and locates its two supports (preservation, reconciliation).

    Epistemic & alethic marks

    • E: Derived, FT — the corollaries (this among them) follow from the axiom by inference, contestable by declining the axiom.
    • A: Aspires to map a real constraint on any meaning-bearing system — one of the four faces of the absence-of-possession condition.

    Distinctions & cautions

    Continuity is NOT perfect retention or infallible memory — the preservation it requires explicitly forbids pretending infallibility. The Reconciliation it depends on is a coupled controller, not a sum (Part III), so continuity must not be read as a mere addition of Apology and Gratitude.
