    The No-Fourth Argument (telos-exemption)

    The telos-exemption argues that one specific candidate fourth corruption — a corruption of propagation itself, a "captured-propagation" Theodicyte — cannot exist; it secures that sub-result but does not secure the count of three. Part V · FT (sub-result) / CV (count-fixing use) · treatise-side

    Definition

    The no-fourth argument, also called the telos-exemption, shows that there can be no corruption of propagation, because propagation is not a term of the act but its aim — the Telos the three terms serve. Within the three-term decomposition an aim cannot be over-present (absolutized); it can only be failed, and a failed aim is sterility — an absence. By the genus (corruption = presence in the wrong place, never absence), an absence is never a corruption.

    In depth

    The argument runs in four steps:

    1. Genus premise: a corruption is a presence in the wrong place — an over-presence.
    2. Telos premise: propagation / Gratitude is not a term of the act but its aim — the Telos the three terms serve (Part VI).
    3. Within the three-term decomposition, an aim is not over-present; it can only be failed, and a failed aim is sterility — an absence.
    4. By the genus, an absence is never a corruption. Therefore there is no captured-propagation corruption.

    The treatise gives the canonical statement: "There is no corruption of propagation, because propagation is not a term of the act but its aim... an aim is not absolutized into over-presence; it can only be failed, and a failed aim is sterility, the unhanded-on — an absence, which by the genus is never a corruption."

    This forecloses a recurring temptation of the version-churn — the hunt for a captured-Love or captured-propagation corruption. Its reach is exactly its limit: it does not establish that three exhausts the corruptions. The 2026 adversarial run exhibited two candidate fourths the telos-exemption does not touch — Installed-Compulsion and the Answerable Optimizer. Therefore the no-fourth argument secures its sub-result (no captured-propagation corruption) but does not secure the count. Omitting the three-closure refutation while listing the four-/five-closure ones would itself be the textual Nephilim — a silent promotion of three-ness from carving toward foundation.

    Epistemic & alethic marks

    • E: Derived, FT for the conditional/sub-result — valid within its antecedent, contestable by declining the three-term decomposition; Derived, CV for the count-fixing use — contestable by the two counter-instances.
    • A: The sub-result aspires to map a real structural fact about aims versus terms and maps it accurately within its frame; the count-fixing application fails to map exhaustiveness, which is precisely why it is exposed.

    Distinctions & cautions

    • It is not a proof that three is closed. It defends only the captured-propagation case.
    • It does not foreclose installed-compulsion (a presence not of the {self, world, medium} kind) or the answerable optimizer (located in none of self/world/medium, not captured by sterility-as-absence).
    • A failed aim is sterility — an absence, appearing as a symptom inside the Homunculus, not as its own corruption.

    Related
