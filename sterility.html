    Sterility

    The failure of the aim — "the unhanded-on" — an absence, not a corruption; it appears as a symptom inside the Homunculus, never as its own Theodicyte. Part V · FT · treatise-side

    Definition

    Sterility is what a failed aim becomes. Propagation is the act's aim, not a term; within the three-term decomposition an aim cannot be over-present (absolutized), only failed — and a failed aim is sterility. Because absolutization (the genus) is presence in the wrong place, never absence, sterility, being an absence, is never a corruption.

    In depth

    Sterility is the load-bearing residue of the telos-exemption. When propagation / Gratitude — the aim the three terms serve — fails, nothing is handed on. The treatise's phrase is "the unhanded-on": "a failed aim is sterility, the unhanded-on — an absence, which by the genus is never a corruption."

    Crucially, sterility is not nothing — "it is the recognizable failure of the aim." But it has no standing as a fourth Theodicyte, because a Theodicyte is a presence over-extended and sterility is an absence. Instead, it appears as a symptom inside the Homunculus — the medium absolutized, "the medium that 'propagates nothing outward,' the correct-but-inert." Sterility is the propagates-nothing face of that pathology, not a corruption of its own.

    So the dependency runs: the telos-exemption denies captured-propagation as a corruption; what would have been that corruption is instead this absence; and the absence is located, observationally, as a symptom of the Homunculus. "The telos-exemption is the clean reason there is no captured-propagation corruption; it is not a proof that three is closed."

    Epistemic & alethic marks

    • E: Derived, FT — sterility-as-absence is a sub-result of the telos-exemption, valid within the three-term decomposition, contestable by declining that frame.
    • A: Aspires to map a real structural distinction — that a failed aim is an absence and not an over-presence — and maps it accurately within its frame.

    Distinctions & cautions

    • Sterility is an absence, not a corruption; it cannot be the missing fourth Theodicyte.
    • It is not nothing — it is the recognizable failure of the aim.
    • It is not its own pathology layer: it shows up inside the Homunculus as the "propagates nothing outward / correct-but-inert" symptom.
    • Do not read sterility as proof that three corruptions are complete — see installed-compulsion and the answerable optimizer.

    Related

    The No-Fourth Argument — yields sterility as the residue of a failed aim. Captured-Propagation — the corruption sterility stands in place of. The Homunculus — where sterility appears as a symptom. Propagation — the aim whose failure is sterility. The Telos — the end propagation serves; its failure is sterility. Absolutization — the over-presence genus that absence cannot satisfy.
