    Fear = (away, boundary violation)

    Away-movement that reduces boundary violation — the discrepancy between current exposure and acceptable protection against threat. Part II · AC · canonical

    Definition

    Fear = (away, boundary violation). It is a predicate binding: the primitive away bound under the regulated error signal boundary violation. Fear is away-movement that reduces boundary violation — the discrepancy between current exposure and acceptable protection against threat. The controller drives that discrepancy toward zero.

    In depth

    Fear is the binding B(away) = (away, boundary violation). The actuator is the directional operator away (excludes or decreases coupling with a signification); the controller is named "Fear"; the error signal is boundary violation. As with every binding, the accuracy method is to extract the regulated error, not the surface delivery channel.

    Fear's error signal carries a correction-history: prior editions (v10, v11) invented "emotion-generator" axes — {toward, away} × {relation, integrity} — and on that spurious carving Fear's error was demoted to "integrity." There are no such axes in the corpus. The invented axes are withdrawn, and Fear's regulated error reverts to boundary violation.

    Fear is a cornerstone binding and an ingredient of Submission (with Love). The fear-guard governs its composite role: Fear is an ingredient of Submission and the object that Reconciliation reconciles, but never an ingredient of Reconciliation.

    Epistemic & alethic marks

    • E: Derived, AC — fixed verbatim by the controlling authority; contestable by contesting the authority.
    • A: The binding aspires to map the regulated error actually driving the named emotion; extract the regulated error, not the surface delivery channel.

    Distinctions & cautions

    Fear is NOT a primitive (treating it as one is the cardinal error). Its error signal is boundary violation, NOT "integrity" — that axis was a hallucination and is withdrawn. Fear is never an ingredient of Reconciliation (the fear-guard).
