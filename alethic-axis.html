    The Alethic Axis

    A second, orthogonal axis measuring how accurately a claim maps reality — wholly independent of contestability, so that a maximally contestable claim can be maximally accurate; contestable ≠ wrong. Part 0a · n-a (the axis itself) · canonical

    Definition

    Epistemic status answers how a claim is held: that it is Derived, and at which contestability tier. It says nothing at all about how accurately the claim maps reality. That is a second, orthogonal question — the alethic axis. A claim can be maximally contestable on the epistemic axis and maximally accurate on the alethic axis, with no tension between the two, because the axes measure different things.

    In depth

    The worked examples: gravity-the-law is a maximally contestable derivation — every term in it is revisable, and physics expects to be proven wrong about it — and it maps the world about as accurately as anything we have. The primes are tautological within arithmetic and map a real regularity of the world. So: contestable does not mean probably-wrong, and it does not mean merely-optional; it means holdable only in a way that stays open to refutation. Accuracy is a separate virtue a contestable claim may have in full.

    What does the alethic axis aspire to, given the axiom? Not possession. The axiom denies any participant the direct possession of the case; the best any claim can do is be a semblance that participates in what it cannot exhaust — an accurate mapping that points truly without owning its object. That ceiling — accurate participation without possession — is not a defect of this framework but the universal condition, applying to physics exactly as it applies here. The framework thus ends, by its own lights, in the same epistemic boat as physics: derived, contestable, accuracy-aspiring, possession-renouncing.

    The alethic axis is not a separate doctrine bolted on: VLS is itself the alethic axis made into doctrine — "a semblance that participates in what it cannot exhaust" is exactly the ceiling this axis names.

    Epistemic & alethic marks

    Exposition / structural rule — the alethic axis is the second mark column of the two-mark system. It is the apparatus by which each load-bearing claim states its accuracy-aspiration; it is not itself a marked world-claim.

    Distinctions & cautions

    • Orthogonal to the epistemic axis: position on one predicts nothing about position on the other.
    • The deflation from "forced" to "derived" touches only the epistemic axis; it leaves the mirror (accuracy aspiration) entirely intact.
    • "Contestable" must NEVER be read as "probably wrong" or "merely optional" — the precise misreading this axis exists to prevent.
