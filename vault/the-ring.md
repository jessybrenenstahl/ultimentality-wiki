# The Ring (aphoristic maximal incomplete set)

> **The five SPLCW entities read not as a row but as a recursive, non-terminating ring — maximal (five is everything it can hold) and incomplete (open at the act, always mid-turn).**
> *Part IV · CV (carving) · treatise-side*

## Definition
The [[epigraph|epigraph]]'s figure is **not a row but a ring**. The five entities form a recursive loop that does not terminate — the **aphoristic maximal incomplete set**: *maximal*, because five is everything the ring can hold; *incomplete*, because the ring is open at the act, always mid-turn.

## In depth
The ring's closure-without-termination runs: pinning needs the ladder, the ladder rests on the floor, the floor takes the nails, the nails are driven by the hammer, the hammer completes the pinning, and the pinning begins again. Because *pinning* is a gerund — an act in the present progressive — the figure is never finished; the [[sculptor|Sculptor]] is the fifth element that holds the ring open. (Mapping the nouns and the verb to the five entities is the work of the [[epigraph|epigraph]].)

"Maximal incomplete set" is precise on both terms: maximal means the ring holds the full complement of five and no sixth; incomplete means it never closes, because there is always an act underway. To call this figure **"complete and closed" is itself to commit the deepest corruption** — the [[absolutization|absolutization]] of the [[textual-nephilim|Nephilim]] — *in the very act of describing the figure that refuses it.*

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, treatise-side CV (carving) — a reading of an aphorism, contestable by counter-instance or by declining the reading.
- **A:** The one-to-one mapping aspires to map the five faculties onto the figure illuminatingly; it is one apt construal, not a possession of the aphorism's meaning.

## Distinctions & cautions
- **Not a row** and **not a linear pipeline**: the ring recurs and does not terminate.
- **Never "complete and closed":** asserting closure of the ring enacts the deepest corruption — self-certification — in describing it.
- *Maximal* and *incomplete* are not in tension: the ring is full at five yet open at the act.
