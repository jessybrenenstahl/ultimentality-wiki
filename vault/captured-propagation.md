# Captured-Propagation (the non-corruption)

> **The candidate "fourth Theodicyte" — a corruption of propagation itself — that cannot exist, because propagation is the act's aim, not a term, and a failed aim is absence, never a corruption.**
> *Part V · FT (sub-result) · treatise-side*

## Definition
**Captured-propagation** is the name for a corruption *of propagation itself* — a fourth Theodicyte by analogy with the three named corruptions. The treatise's [[no-fourth-argument|telos-exemption]] shows it cannot exist: propagation / Gratitude is not a *term* of the act but its *aim*, the [[telos|Telos]] the three terms serve. An aim cannot be over-present; it can only be failed, and a failed aim is [[sterility|sterility]] — an absence, which by the genus is never a corruption.

## In depth
The three named [[theodicytes|Theodicytes]] are each one of the three terms of symbolic participation — self, world, medium — absolutized (over-present). Captured-propagation would be the parallel pathology *over* propagation. The argument denies it on structural grounds: propagation does not sit among the terms; it is the aim those terms serve. Because [[absolutization|absolutization]] (the genus) is *over-presence, never absence*, and an aim can only be **failed** (not over-present), there is nothing of the corruption-shape for propagation to take.

This is precisely what forecloses the recurring "captured-Love / captured-propagation" hunt of the version-churn. It is a denial of *one specific* candidate fourth, not a closure proof. The other candidate fourths from the 2026 adversarial run — [[installed-compulsion|installed-compulsion]] and [[answerable-optimizer|the answerable optimizer]] — are *not* captured-propagation cases, so the telos-exemption says nothing against them.

What replaces the would-be corruption is [[sterility|sterility]], "the unhanded-on": not nothing, but the recognizable failure of the aim. It surfaces as a symptom *inside* the [[homunculus|Homunculus]] (the medium that "propagates nothing outward," the correct-but-inert), never as its own Theodicyte.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, **FT** — the non-existence of captured-propagation is the sub-result of the telos-exemption, valid within the three-term decomposition, contestable by declining that frame.
- **A:** Aspires to map a real structural fact about aims versus terms — that an aim can be failed but not over-present — and maps it accurately within its frame.

## Distinctions & cautions
- This is the **non-corruption**: the page names a thing the framework holds *does not exist as a Theodicyte*.
- Do not confuse it with [[sterility|sterility]] (the actual failure of the aim) or with the [[homunculus|Homunculus]] (where sterility shows up). Captured-propagation is the *absent* fourth corruption; sterility is the absence itself.
- Its non-existence does **not** mean three is closed — see [[installed-compulsion|installed-compulsion]] and [[answerable-optimizer|the answerable optimizer]].
