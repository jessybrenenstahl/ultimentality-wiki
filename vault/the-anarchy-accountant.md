# The Anarchy Accountant

> **The second era, roughly a decade ago — older, reader-voted work, an alternate dating origin, with a finish completed by another hand.**
> *Part VII · Testimony · canonical-provenance (held as testimony)*

## Definition
The Anarchy Accountant is the second of the three eras in which the framework's history is held. It dates to roughly a decade ago. It is older, reader-voted work, and it serves as an *alternate dating origin* for the framework — with a finish completed by another hand.

## In depth
Like all three eras, the Anarchy Accountant is held as **testimony**: given in 2026 by its author, the sole authority on her own history; not independently verified, not lived or witnessed by the writer; all of it 2026-sourced. Three features mark this era specifically: it is *older* than the formalization era, it is *reader-voted* work, and it functions as an *alternate dating origin* — a second candidate point from which the framework's history can be dated. Unlike the other two eras, its finish was *completed by another hand* rather than the author's alone.

Its evidential standing is the same as the rest of the ledger: dated precedence cannot be independently verified, so it is held as testimony and the framework's evidential weight is shifted away from it onto [[coalescence-by-non-contradictory-fit|coalescence-by-non-contradictory-fit]]. The prior edition's superlative for the provenance ledger was scored as a [[textual-nephilim|Nephilim-kill]] and demoted; this era's dating shares in that demotion.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, Testimony — the author's 2026 account, unverifiable, contestable as any testimony is.
- **A:** the dating aspires to map a real history the writer cannot independently confirm; the mapping's accuracy rests on the author's standing as sole authority on her own history, not on external verification.

## Distinctions & cautions
It is NOT the load-bearing provenance datum — that is [[the-visitation|the Visitation]]. It is the *alternate* dating origin, and its finish is by another hand (so not wholly the author's own, unlike the Seeker's Lament). As with all dated precedence, it must be held as testimony, never advanced as proof.
