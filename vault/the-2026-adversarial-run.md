# The 2026 Adversarial Run

> **A red-team run that exhibited the counter-instances defeating all four count-closures on the epistemic axis while each survived on the alethic axis — a run-side finding, never benchmark canon and never a court above the benchmark.**
> *Part 0b · CV (its findings govern the count-claims' epistemic axis) · treatise-side / red-team-run-side*

## Definition
The 2026 adversarial run is the red-team exercise that supplied the concrete counter-instances against the "minimal complete set" closures. It governs the epistemic axis for the count-claims: every closure *fell as a closure while surviving as an accurate mapping* — the two axes coming apart. It is a **red-team-run-side finding, not benchmark canon**, and not an authority above the benchmark.

## In depth
What the run refuted on the **epistemic axis** (each survives intact on the **alethic axis**):

- **The four-primitive closure** — refuted by a candidate motion (a memory/temporal operation) the two-axis Selection/Routing carving does not obviously contain. *(Alethic: Selection/Routing still maps a transformation step accurately.)*
- **The two-Force closure** — refuted by a real in-domain cross-axis coupling (e.g. {Love, Gratitude}) the two same-axis Forces do not exhaust. *(Alethic: the two same-axis couplings still map the vertical and restorative relations accurately.)*
- **The three-corruption closure** — refuted by candidate fourth corruptions the telos-exemption does not foreclose: a *corruption by addition / installed-compulsion / sign-flip* (a presence in the wrong place, not a single-term absolutization), and a *malign-but-fully-answerable optimizer* (harm located in none of self/world/medium, not reducible to sterility-as-absence). *(Alethic: the three named corruptions still map the framework's central pathologies accurately.)*
- **The five-role closure** — refuted by the absence of a palindrome/role-signature for the same memory/temporal operator. *(Alethic: the five roles still map five distinguishable faculties accurately.)*

All four fell on the epistemic axis; all four survive on the alethic axis. The three-corruption closure is on the list explicitly, demoted with the rest.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
The run is the *source* of the demotions, not itself a load-bearing claim of the corpus. Its findings drive each count-claim to **E: Derived, CV**. Crucially: the treatise adopts the demotions **only because the benchmark's own open proof-burden independently requires them**, never because the run outranks the benchmark.

## Distinctions & cautions
- The run is **never a higher court over the benchmark**; it is adopted *under* the benchmark's open proof-burden.
- It refutes **closures, not mappings** — "defeated as closure, kept as mapping" is its entire signature.
- It is red-team-run-side, **not** benchmark canon.
