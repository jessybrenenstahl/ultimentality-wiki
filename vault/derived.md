# Derived (the single epistemic status)

> **Every claim the treatise makes is marked with exactly one epistemic status — Derived; nothing is forced, given, foundational, axiomatic-as-bedrock, or self-evident.**
> *Part 0a · n-a (the status itself) · canonical*

## Definition
This treatise marks every claim it makes with exactly one epistemic status: **Derived**. Nothing in it is "forced," "given," "foundational," "axiomatic-as-bedrock," or "self-evident." The axiom is derived; the primitives are derived; the bindings, definitions, theorems, the constitutive identification of the Telos, and the very logical frame they are stated in — all derived. This is not a concession wrung from the framework; it is *entailed by the framework's own axiom*.

## In depth
The entailment runs through [[axiom|the axiom]]: all access is symbolic, with no extra-symbolic channel available to a participant *as* participant. If nothing is *given* outside the symbolic, then nothing is given at all — every claim a participant can make or hold is arrived at *through* symbolic transformation, which is to say, derived. To exempt any one claim — to stamp it "forced" or "foundational" — is to assert a privileged extra-symbolic access for that claim alone, which the axiom forbids. So the no-foundation rule is not imposed *on* the framework from outside; it falls *out* of it, and a text that exempts even its own axiom from derivation has contradicted the axiom it exempts.

The single Derived status does **not** flatten all claims to equal contestability. Derivations differ in *how* they can be contested, along [[contestability-gradient|the contestability gradient]] (FT → CV → AC). Prior editions ran a two-tier *Forced / Derived* ledger; this edition removes the "Forced" tier outright, because a "Forced" tier is a standing invitation to [[textual-nephilim|the textual Nephilim]] — a reserved place for derivations allowed to forget they are derivations. Everything is Derived; nothing is exempt; the witness's seat stays empty.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
Exposition / structural rule — the single-status declaration is the *frame* in which load-bearing claims receive their marks, not itself a load-bearing world-mapping claim. It carries the [[two-mark-system|two-mark]] apparatus rather than asserting through it.

## Distinctions & cautions
- "Derived" is NOT a concession or a softening — it is the completion of the framework's own discipline.
- There is exactly one thing that is *not* a derived claim: [[bare-operation-of-inference|the bare operation of inference]] — and it is no foundation either, being a content-free medium, not a term.
- Derived ≠ probably-wrong and ≠ merely-optional; that is the [[alethic-axis|alethic axis's]] separate concern.
