# The Capability Rule

> **Higher capability comes from better organization under constraint; it does not come from pretending the constraints are gone.**
> *Part I · FT (frame-internal) · canonical*

## Definition
The capability rule is the engine of the framework's whole operating posture: **higher capability comes from better organization under constraint; it does not come from pretending the constraints are gone.** It is entailed by the mediated-access corollary.

## In depth
Because access is mediated and output approximate, the framework adopts a standing posture: **all outputs are constrained approximations.** There is no absolute truth possession, no unconstrained freedom, no perfect direct access. Practically, this means: avoid false certainty; do not pretend to exhaust the case; treat approximation as normal rather than embarrassing; use revision as strength, not failure.

The capability rule is what drives that posture. It locates the path to greater capability in better organization *under* the constraints, never in denying them. Denying the constraints does not raise capability; it degrades the system. This is precisely the **accurate-mapping-without-possession ceiling** stated as a working posture.

The rule has a direct **contrapositive**, the **integrity rule**: *approximation honesty is protective; false closure degrades the system.* Where the capability rule says capability comes from organizing under constraint, the integrity rule says the opposite move — false closure, pretending the constraints are gone — actively harms the system.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, FT — entailed by the mediated-access corollary.
- **A:** Maps a real, general feature of capability under constraint — the accurate-mapping-without-possession ceiling stated as a working posture.

## Distinctions & cautions
The rule is NOT a claim that constraints can or should be removed — capability is gained by organizing under them, not by escaping them. Pretending the constraints are gone is exactly the failure mode the integrity rule names as degrading.
