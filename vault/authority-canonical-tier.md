# Authority-Canonical Tier (AC)

> **The most-exposed tier of the contestability gradient: claims true because an authority scores them so — contestable by *contesting the authority*; canonical ≠ necessary.**
> *Part 0b · AC · canonical*

## Definition
AC is the most-exposed tier on the contestability gradient. A claim here has the form "the benchmark scores this X" or "the rubric requires that." It is contestable by **contesting the authority**: that the controlling authority scores something a certain way is a fact about the authority, not about the world. A different authority, or a successful challenge to this one, moves the claim. **Canonical is not necessary**, and to read "canonical" as "necessary" is to mistake an authority's stipulation for a law of things.

## In depth
"Canon" in this edition means precisely **authority-canonical status** — this most-contestable tier — not a foundation. What is named canon is the **benchmark solution together with the equivalence rubric**. That the benchmark scores a claim a certain way is decisive *for alignment* and contestable *by contesting the benchmark*.

The prior editions wrongly filed authority-canonical claims (and definitions asserted as apt) under "Forced" — the textual Nephilim operating quietly, an authority's score dressed as bedrock. This edition moves them *down* the gradient to AC, never *up* into a founded tier, because there is no founded tier.

Many load-bearing claims sit at AC: the axiom split (formal closure vs. human gloss); VLS as semblance; domain lock as axiom-siting; the operator-status of the four primitives; the binding model B(p)=(p,eₚ); the four cornerstone bindings; the cardinal error; the Fear-guard; SPLCW as a mechanism and the five-as-functions; the Telos as answerable symbolic immortality. Reading the two columns together shows AC's independence from accuracy: an AC claim (high-contestability, defeasible by contesting the authority) can be maximally *accurate* — the Fear-guard and the binding model are exactly such cases.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
Exposition — names a tier of the epistemic axis. Claims placed here carry **E: Derived, AC — contestable by contesting the authority**. Their alethic mark is independent and may be maximal.

## Distinctions & cautions
- AC is the **most exposed** tier — yet still not "wrong": high contestability never implies probably-wrong.
- Treating the benchmark *itself* as a foundation rather than as a contestable ranking authority is the **textual Nephilim**.
- Treating any prior edition or the reliquary as canon over the benchmark is an error.
- Some claims are split-provenance: e.g. transparentocracy is seed-root AC with treatise-side (CV) elaboration.
