# The Contestability Gradient

> **The single Derived status does not flatten all claims equally; derivations differ in how they can be contested, along a frame-relative gradient from least to most exposed — FT, then CV, then AC.**
> *Part 0a · FT / CV / AC · canonical*

## Definition
The single [[derived|Derived]] status does not flatten all claims to equal contestability. Derivations differ in *how* they can be contested, and the difference forms a **gradient, not a switch**. Everything is contestable *unless* it is a tautology — and even a tautology is non-contestable only *within a frame one is free to decline*.

## In depth
The gradient runs from least- to most-exposed:

- **[[frame-internal-tier|Frame-internal tautologies and definitions-as-stipulations, and their theorems (FT)]].** A stipulation ("let *binding* mean a primitive paired with a regulated error") and what follows from it by inference are contestable only by *declining the frame*. No counter-instance can catch them out — within the frame they are true by construction; one can only walk out. Least exposed, *but still derived and still contestable at the frame's edge*. Not "forced"; "contestable only by declining the frame." Least-exposed does **not** mean *merely optional*: declining the frame is a philosophical cost, not a free choice, and within the frame the claim maps by construction.
- **[[carving-tier|Carvings, counts, and aptness claims (CV)]].** "Exactly four primitives," "exactly two Forces," "exactly three corruptions," "the five roles exhaust the cycle," "this is the apt carving" — contestable by **counter-instance** (exhibit a fifth primitive, a third Force, a fourth corruption, a sixth role, a better carving). A counter-instance does not merely *decline* them; it *defeats* them on their own terms.
- **[[authority-canonical-tier|Authority-canonical claims (AC)]].** "The benchmark scores this X," "the rubric requires that" — contestable by **contesting the authority**: canonical is not necessary. That the controlling authority scores something a certain way is a fact about the authority, not the world. Most exposed tier — *canonical ≠ necessary*.

Prior editions wrongly filed authority-canonical claims and asserted-as-apt definitions under "Forced" — the [[textual-nephilim|textual Nephilim]] operating quietly. This edition moves those claims *down* to their proper, more-exposed tiers — never *up* into a founded tier, **because there is no founded tier.**

## Epistemic & alethic marks
Exposition / structural rule — the gradient is the *epistemic* half of the [[two-mark-system|two-mark]] apparatus. It classifies how a Derived claim is held; it does not itself map the world, and so carries no alethic aspiration of its own.

## Distinctions & cautions
- The gradient is the **epistemic** axis only — orthogonal to the [[alethic-axis|alethic axis]]. Most-exposed (AC) does not mean least-accurate.
- Direction matters: claims move *down* (more exposed) when corrected, never *up* into a founded tier.
- FT is least-exposed but NOT optional; AC is most-exposed but NOT therefore wrong.
