# Absolutization (the genus)

> **A corruption is a term of the act absolutized — a single function isolated and inflated until it crowds out the rest and takes itself for the whole.**
> *Part V · AC · canonical (genus fixed by the authority)*

## Definition
Absolutization is the **genus** of all three Theodicytes. A corruption is not a missing function but one function **over-present**: isolated, inflated, taking itself for the whole. Its decisive property: *the corruptions are not absences but presences in the wrong place — one function over-present, never a missing one.* An absolutized function does not vanish; it becomes an **attractor that captures the system into a stable, self-maintaining pathology.**

## In depth
The genus matches the canon at the level of mechanism: *meaning corrupts when any function absolutizes itself;* corruption occurs when a role or affective predicate becomes distorted, isolated, *or absolutized.*

**Self-application.** The [[textual-nephilim|textual Nephilim]] of the Preamble — a claim absolutized into a "foundation" — is itself a presence in the wrong place: a derivation over-present, inflated past its station into bedrock. The no-foundation rule is the genus turned on the page. A "Forced" tier is not a missing piece the framework lacks; it is one tier *over-present*, a derivation crowding out its own derived status. Removing it is not subtracting content; it is correcting an over-presence.

**Why *absolutization* and not *severance*.** A prior edition read the three as "severances of a controller from its regulators" — a *cutting-off* that leans toward the privation/absence framing the corpus explicitly disavows. This edition reverts it: severance was a reconstruction from the control-theoretic signpost, not the corpus's own ontology. **The over-presence *is* the corruption;** the suppression of the rest is its downstream consequence, not a second mechanism. The seed's surface form ("X dominates; the others are suppressed") is the same genus seen from the primitive layer.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, AC — the over-presence genus is fixed by the authority; contestable by contesting the authority.
- **A:** The genus aspires to map a real mechanism — that these pathologies are presences in the wrong place, not privations — and maps it accurately; the absence-reading would misdescribe the mechanism.

## Distinctions & cautions
Absolutization is **over-presence, not privation**: read the corruptions as absences and you misdescribe the mechanism. This is exactly why a *failed aim* (sterility) is never a corruption — an absence cannot be an over-presence ([[no-fourth-argument|the no-fourth argument]]). The genus applies to the framework's own page: stamping any claim "foundational" is itself the genus enacted.
