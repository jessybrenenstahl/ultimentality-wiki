# Conceptual Derivatives

> **Towardness, awayness, inwardness, outwardness — human-readable nouns abstracted from the bound primitives; not themselves primitive.**
> *Part II · AC · canonical*

## Definition
The **conceptual derivatives** are *towardness, awayness, inwardness, outwardness*. They are **human-readable nouns abstracted from the bound primitives**. They are **not primitive**, and the seed flags them so. They sit on the derivational order one rung after the predicate bindings — *directional primitives → predicate bindings → **conceptual derivatives** → composites → SPLCW operator chain → failure states → applications.*

## In depth
Where the directional primitives are bare, unexperienced operators ([[toward|toward]], [[away|away]], [[loop-back|loop-back]], [[propagation|propagation]]) and the bindings pair an actuator with a regulated error, the conceptual derivatives are the *nominalized, human-readable* abstractions drawn from those bound primitives. They give a readable vocabulary — towardness, awayness, inwardness, outwardness — without claiming the foundational status of the operators they derive from.

Their nounhood is the whole point: they are derivatives, downstream of the bound primitives, and the seed explicitly flags them as non-primitive. Reading them as primitive would commit a version of the same inversion the layer most guards against.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
Exposition — carries no load-bearing mark of its own beyond the layer's shared frame. (The derivatives are named within the authority-canonical binding passage; the seed flags their non-primitive status. They add no independent E/A claim of their own: their epistemic weight is that of the bindings they abstract from.)

## Distinctions & cautions
Conceptual derivatives are NOT primitives and NOT bindings — they are human-readable nouns abstracted from the bound primitives. "Inwardness / outwardness" track the [[routing-axis|Routing axis]] (loop-back / propagation); "towardness / awayness" track the [[selection-axis|Selection axis]] (toward / away). Treating a derivative as primitive participates in the inversion that [[cardinal-error|the cardinal error]] names.
