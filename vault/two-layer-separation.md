# Two-Layer Separation

> **The corpus keeps two separate failure layers — three system-level Theodicytes (self/world/medium) and five SPLCW role-captures — and five does not factor into three, so no forced partition between them can hold.**
> *Part V · AC (the distinction & coalition relation) / CV (the specific edges) · canonical (distinction) + treatise-side (correction)*

## Definition
**Two-layer separation** is the structural correction that the framework holds **two distinct failure registers**: **Layer A**, the three named system-level [[theodicytes|Theodicytes]], each defined by which non-telos term over-dominates at the self/world/medium layer; and **Layer B**, the five [[splcw|SPLCW]] role-captures, one failure per role internal to the meaning-processing cycle. These are *complementary* layers, not a relabeling — and **five does not factor into three.**

## In depth
The single most important structural correction in this part is that the version-churn came from *wrongly fusing* the two layers — trying to force the five role-captures into a clean partition under the three Theodicytes.

**Layer A — three system-level Theodicytes**, each by which non-telos term over-dominates, read at the self/world/medium layer: [[spectre|Spectre]] (world), [[nephilim|Nephilim]] (self), [[homunculus|Homunculus]] (medium).

**Layer B — five SPLCW role-captures**, one failure per role: [[warden|Warden]] → rigidity; [[captive|Captive]] → collapse and confusion; [[logician|Logician]] → sterile flattening; [[poet|Poet]] → decorative drift; [[sculptor|Sculptor]] → premature action.

Because there are five roles and three Theodicytes, "**five does not factor into three** — which is exactly why any forced partition produces a contradiction somewhere." The canon confirms the layers are distinct and that role → Theodicyte is a **coalition relation, not an identity** ([[role-corruption-affinities|Spectre = Warden + Sculptor; Nephilim = Captive + Logician; Homunculus = Poet]]), and is explicit that this mapping *evolved and was corrected* over time — direct evidence that it is a **revisable affinity, not a settled derivation.** The "Logician/Poet flip" that drove the churn "is entirely an artifact of trying to align Layer B one-to-one under Layer A; grant that the alignment is a *soft affinity* rather than a partition, and there is nothing to flip."

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** The two-layer distinction and the coalition relation are Derived, **AC**; the specific edges are Derived, **CV** soft affinities (contestable by counter-instance).
- **A:** The two layers aspire to map two genuinely distinct failure registers, and the non-factoring of five into three is the accurate reason no clean partition exists.

## Distinctions & cautions
- The layers are **complementary, not a relabeling**; do not collapse Layer B onto Layer A.
- Role → Theodicyte is a **coalition (soft affinity), never an identity or partition** — see [[role-corruption-affinities|Role–Corruption Affinities]].
- The mapping is a **revisable affinity, not a settled derivation** (the canon corrected it over time).
- The "Logician/Poet flip" is an *artifact* of forced one-to-one alignment, dissolved by holding the affinity soft.
