# Fear = (away, boundary violation)

> **Away-movement that reduces boundary violation — the discrepancy between current exposure and acceptable protection against threat.**
> *Part II · AC · canonical*

## Definition
**Fear = (away, boundary violation).** It is a [[predicate-binding|predicate binding]]: the primitive [[away|away]] bound under the regulated error signal **boundary violation**. Fear is away-movement that *reduces boundary violation* — the discrepancy between current exposure and acceptable protection against threat. The controller drives that discrepancy toward zero.

## In depth
Fear is the binding **B(away) = (away, boundary violation)**. The actuator is the directional operator [[away|away]] (excludes or decreases coupling with a signification); the controller is named "Fear"; the error signal is boundary violation. As with every binding, the accuracy method is to extract the regulated error, not the surface delivery channel.

Fear's error signal carries a correction-history: prior editions (v10, v11) invented "emotion-generator" axes — {toward, away} × {relation, **integrity**} — and on that spurious carving Fear's error was demoted to "integrity." **There are no such axes in the corpus.** The invented axes are withdrawn, and Fear's regulated error **reverts to boundary violation**.

Fear is a cornerstone binding and an ingredient of [[submission|Submission]] (with [[love|Love]]). The **fear-guard** governs its composite role: Fear is an ingredient of Submission and the object that Reconciliation reconciles, but **never** an ingredient of [[reconciliation|Reconciliation]].

## Distinctions & cautions
Fear is NOT a primitive (treating it as one is [[cardinal-error|the cardinal error]]). Its error signal is **boundary violation**, NOT "integrity" — that axis was a hallucination and is withdrawn. Fear is never an ingredient of Reconciliation ([[fear-guard|the fear-guard]]).
