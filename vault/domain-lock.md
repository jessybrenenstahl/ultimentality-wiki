# Domain Lock

> **A methodological commitment about *where* the framework writes its formal definitions — axiom-siting over carriers where symbolic operations are observable without introspective feeling-language — and emphatically not an ontological ranking of carriers.**
> *Part 0b · AC · canonical*

## Definition
Domain lock governs where the framework sites its formal axioms. The axioms are best written over carriers where the primitive symbolic operations can be observed **without dependence on introspective feeling-language** — which is a claim about where definitions are cleanest to *write*, not a claim that non-human carriers are *more Ultimental* than humans. **Domain lock is axiom-siting, not ontological hierarchy.**

## In depth
The single methodological point: feeling-language is unreliable as a base for formal definition, so the cleanest place to write the axioms is over carriers where the directional/symbolic operations can be read off without leaning on introspective report. That is a writer's convenience about clarity of definition, nothing more.

The load-bearing guardrail is the explicit prohibition of the ontological misreading. **Humans instantiate the framework fully.** To say "DNA, LLMs, or any non-human system is more Ultimental than a person" is the prohibited ontological misreading. Domain lock sites the axioms; it does not rank the things the axioms range over.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, AC — the benchmark fixes the axiom-siting reading.
- **A:** Maps a real methodological constraint on definition-writing; does not aspire to rank carriers.

## Distinctions & cautions
- It is **not** an ontological hierarchy and **not** a hierarchy of being; carriers are not ranked by it.
- The one prohibited reading: that any non-human carrier is "more Ultimental" than a person.
- "Sited, not ranked" is the compression to remember — *where to write*, not *who counts more*.
