# Sterility

> **The failure of the aim — "the unhanded-on" — an absence, not a corruption; it appears as a symptom inside the Homunculus, never as its own Theodicyte.**
> *Part V · FT · treatise-side*

## Definition
**Sterility** is what a failed aim becomes. Propagation is the act's aim, not a term; within the three-term decomposition an aim cannot be over-present (absolutized), only **failed** — and a failed aim is sterility. Because [[absolutization|absolutization]] (the genus) is *presence in the wrong place, never absence*, sterility, being an absence, is **never a corruption**.

## In depth
Sterility is the load-bearing residue of the [[no-fourth-argument|telos-exemption]]. When [[propagation|propagation]] / Gratitude — the aim the three terms serve — fails, nothing is handed on. The treatise's phrase is "the unhanded-on": *"a failed aim is sterility, the unhanded-on — an absence, which by the genus is never a corruption."*

Crucially, **sterility is not nothing** — "it is the recognizable failure of the aim." But it has no standing as a fourth [[theodicytes|Theodicyte]], because a Theodicyte is a *presence* over-extended and sterility is an *absence*. Instead, **it appears as a symptom inside the [[homunculus|Homunculus]]** — the medium absolutized, "the medium that 'propagates nothing outward,' the correct-but-inert." Sterility is the *propagates-nothing* face of that pathology, not a corruption of its own.

So the dependency runs: the telos-exemption denies [[captured-propagation|captured-propagation]] as a corruption; what would have been that corruption is instead this absence; and the absence is located, observationally, as a symptom of the Homunculus. "The telos-exemption is the clean reason there is no captured-*propagation* corruption; it is not a proof that three is closed."

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, **FT** — sterility-as-absence is a sub-result of the telos-exemption, valid within the three-term decomposition, contestable by declining that frame.
- **A:** Aspires to map a real structural distinction — that a failed aim is an absence and not an over-presence — and maps it accurately within its frame.

## Distinctions & cautions
- Sterility is **an absence, not a corruption**; it cannot be the missing fourth Theodicyte.
- It is **not nothing** — it is the recognizable failure of the aim.
- It is **not** its own pathology layer: it shows up *inside* the [[homunculus|Homunculus]] as the "propagates nothing outward / correct-but-inert" symptom.
- Do not read sterility as proof that three corruptions are complete — see [[installed-compulsion|installed-compulsion]] and [[answerable-optimizer|the answerable optimizer]].
