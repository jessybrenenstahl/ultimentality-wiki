# The Directional Primitives

> **Four bare directional operators — toward, away, loop-back, propagation — primitive directional structures, not feelings and not metaphors.**
> *Part II · AC (operator-status and the four semantics) · canonical*

## Definition
Beneath the affective and moral architecture sits a base layer of bare structure. Its first rung is the four directional primitives: **toward, away, loop-back, propagation.** They are *operators*, not feelings — "primitive directional structures, not feelings and not metaphors." The felt qualities of the same names are gloss, carrying no probative weight.

## In depth
The derivational order the corpus protects runs: **directional primitives → predicate bindings → conceptual derivatives → composites → SPLCW operator chain → failure states → applications.** This Part treats the first two rungs and the axes that close them. The four primitives sit at the base.

The seed names the routing pair *inward* and *outward*, with symbols → ← ↺ ↻; the rename to *loop-back* and *propagation* is exact and structure-preserving — terminology, not architecture. The seed's binding rules pre-empt the [[cardinal-error|cardinal error]] directly: *predicates do not define the primitives; primitives are not reducible to the predicates; these are not to be read as ordinary human feelings.*

The four are not emotions, moods, virtues, images, or personality styles. They are directional operators — bare, unexperienced structure. The set is not merely *listed* at four; it is *carved* at four through the [[selection-axis|Selection]] and [[routing-axis|Routing]] axes (see below). One discipline governs the whole layer: Fear, Love, Apology, and Gratitude are [[predicate-binding|predicate bindings]], not primitives.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, AC for the operator-status and the four semantics — fixed by the controlling authority; contestable by contesting the authority.
- **A:** The four definitions aspire to map the elementary directional moves of any transformation step; that the operators are bare and unexperienced is itself the accurate claim, against which the felt gloss has no probative weight.

## Distinctions & cautions
The primitives are NOT the emotions named like them, and not metaphors. The predicate does not define the primitive; the primitive is not reducible to the predicate. Collapsing the operators into the bindings is the [[cardinal-error|cardinal error]], capping any exposition at 72.
