# The Open Proof-Burden

> **The benchmark's own stance that it does not assert the count-closures as proven — the in-house authority basis on which this edition demotes every closure to the carving tier.**
> *Part 0b · AC · canonical*

## Definition
The benchmark itself carries the **open proof-burden**: it does not assert the count-closures as proven. Because the controlling authority leaves the closures unproven, this edition is entitled — on that benchmark authority, held as a contestable ranking authority and not as bedrock — to demote every closure to the contestable carving tier.

## In depth
The open proof-burden is the linchpin of the edition's two visible overrides of the prior ledger-edition: demoting every "minimal complete set" from *closed and forced* to a contestable carving, and abolishing the "Forced" tier. Both demotions rest on the benchmark's own refusal to claim the closures as proven.

This matters for the chain of authority. The 2026 adversarial run *exhibited* the counter-instances, but the treatise adopts those demotions **only because the benchmark's open proof-burden independently requires them** — never because the run is an authority above the benchmark. The run is a red-team-run-side finding, not benchmark canon, and never a higher court. The open proof-burden is what lets the edition act on the run's findings without elevating the run over canon.

It is also the framework's connection to its acceptance gate: the framework's acceptance condition is *survivable refutation*, and the benchmark's open proof-burden is the standing acknowledgment that the closures have not been closed — the door the falsification program walks through.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, AC — a fact about the benchmark authority's own stance, contestable by contesting the benchmark.
- **A:** Maps the real epistemic posture of the controlling authority — that it holds the closures open rather than possessing them as proven.

## Distinctions & cautions
- The open proof-burden is the benchmark's stance, **not** the run's claim; the run never outranks the benchmark.
- It licenses *demotion to carving*, never promotion to a founded tier — there is no founded tier.
- It is why the three-corruption count is demoted on the same footing as all other counts.
