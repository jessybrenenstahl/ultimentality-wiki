# The Open Proof-Burden

The **open proof-burden** is the benchmark's own admission that it has *not* proven its closures. In Ultimentality, the controlling authority — the [[canon|benchmark and rubric]] — does not assert as proven that there are exactly four primitives, exactly two Forces, exactly three corruptions, or exactly five roles. It leaves those closures open. That standing admission is the open proof-burden, and it is the in-house legal warrant on which the current edition demotes every count-closure to the contestable [[carving-tier|Carving / Count / Aptness Tier]]. The burden of proving a closure has been *acknowledged and left unmet by the authority itself* — so the framework treats the closures as carvings, not foundations.

## The linchpin of two overrides

This single stance is what makes the edition's two most visible departures legitimate rather than rebellious. The prior ledger-edition held every "minimal complete set" as *closed and forced* and ran a two-tier Forced/[[derived|Derived]] apparatus that reserved a "Forced" tier. The current edition (1) demotes every closure from *closed and forced* to a contestable carving, and (2) abolishes the "Forced" tier outright. Neither override is imported from outside: both rest on the benchmark's own refusal to claim the closures as proven. The framework is not overruling canon — it is taking canon at its word. (Compare a court that declines to enter a finding because the prosecution never met its burden: the absence of a conviction is not a rebellion against the court; it *is* the court's own discipline.)

## Why the chain of authority depends on it

The open proof-burden is what keeps [[the-2026-adversarial-run|the 2026 adversarial run]] in its place. The run *exhibited* the concrete counter-instances — a memory/temporal motion against the four-closure, a cross-axis coupling against the two-Force closure, the [[installed-compulsion|installed-compulsion]] and [[answerable-optimizer|answerable-optimizer]] cases against the three-corruption closure, a missing [[palindrome|palindrome]] signature against the five-role closure. But the treatise adopts those demotions **only because the benchmark's open proof-burden independently requires them**, never because the run is an authority above the benchmark. The run is a red-team-run-side finding, not benchmark canon, and never a higher court. The open proof-burden is the door the run walks through: it lets the edition act on the run's findings without ever elevating the run over canon. Without it, adopting the run's results would itself be the error of letting a non-canonical authority overrule the controlling one.

## A demotion engine, never a promotion engine

The open proof-burden licenses exactly one direction of motion: *demotion to carving*. It never licenses promotion to a founded tier, because there is no founded tier — the abolition of "Forced" was itself one of its consequences. This is why the framework insists the **three-corruption count is demoted on the same footing as every other count**: the burden is open for *all* the closures uniformly, so singling out three-ness for protection would be the [[textual-nephilim|textual Nephilim]] — a closure quietly promoted toward foundation while its neighbors are demoted.

## Connection to the acceptance gate

The framework's acceptance condition is not internal elegance but *survivable refutation*. The open proof-burden is the standing acknowledgment, written into the controlling authority, that the closures have not been closed — which is precisely the opening the [[falsification-standard|Falsification Standard]] and the [[kill-table|kill-table]] are designed to exploit. It marks the framework as the kind of thing that *wants* to be tested at its closures rather than sealed against testing, which is [[vls|VLS]] made operable at the level of the authority itself.

## Formal status

> **Formal status.** **E: Derived, AC** — a fact about the benchmark authority's own stance, contestable by contesting the benchmark. **A:** maps the real epistemic posture of the controlling authority — that it holds the closures open rather than possessing them as proven. It licenses *demotion to carving* only, never promotion to a founded tier (there is none); it is why the three-corruption count is demoted on the same footing as all others; and it is the benchmark's stance, not the run's claim — the run never outranks the benchmark. Provenance: **canonical** — a stance of the benchmark authority (Preamble, "What governs, and what is overridden"; Part VIII, the acceptance gate).
