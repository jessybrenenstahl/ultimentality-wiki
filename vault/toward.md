# Toward

> **Admits or increases coupling with a signification.**
> *Part II · AC · canonical*

## Definition
**Toward** is one of the four directional primitives. Its operational semantics, authority-canonical: it *admits or increases coupling with a signification.* It sits on the [[selection-axis|Selection]] axis — the domain-side, what is admitted or excluded.

## In depth
Toward is a bare directional operator, not a feeling. With its pair [[away|Away]], it forms the **Selection** axis: the domain-side of a recursive transformation step, governing what a step admits or excludes. Toward is the admitting move — it increases coupling with a signification rather than decreasing it.

It is not an emotion, mood, virtue, image, or personality style. It is unexperienced structure; the felt quality of the same name is gloss with no probative weight. Toward is load-bearing in the next rung: it is the actuator in [[love|Love = (toward, relational gap)]], the toward-coupling that reduces the discrepancy between current separation and desired bond or continuity of coupling. The conceptual derivative *towardness* is a human-readable noun abstracted from the bound primitive — not itself primitive.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, AC — fixed by the controlling authority; contestable by contesting the authority.
- **A:** Aspires to map an elementary directional move of any transformation step (admit / increase coupling); the operator is bare and unexperienced, and that is the accurate claim.

## Distinctions & cautions
Toward is NOT Love. Love is a [[predicate-binding|predicate binding]] over toward; the predicate does not define the primitive. Reading toward as the emotion, or as a metaphor, inverts the layers — the [[cardinal-error|cardinal error]]. Toward (Selection) does not collapse into the [[routing-axis|Routing]] primitives: the four combinations are behaviorally distinct.
