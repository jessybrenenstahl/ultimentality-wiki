# VLS (Verietliberisimilitude)

> **VLS is the condition in which truth and freedom are encountered as *semblances* rather than *possessions*.**
> *Part I · AC (load-bearing claim) / CV (descriptive carvings) · canonical core with seed-side carvings*

## Definition
VLS — Verietliberisimilitude — is "the semblance of truth and freedom." Its one load-bearing claim is that truth and freedom are met as semblances, not possessions: you do not possess truth or freedom, but a semblance is not a lie. A semblance is an appearance that participates in what it cannot exhaust. This is the framework's most direct statement of the universal no-possession condition.

## In depth
The seed states VLS in its deepest and most idiosyncratic passage: *"VLS is the semblance of truth and freedom. Truth and freedom are a shattered unity. They are the 0 outside of experience which we approximate as 1 and 0, because we either believe one or the other. To believe in absolute truth and absolute freedom simultaneously is incoherent."*

Within this, **exactly one claim is load-bearing** — the semblance-not-possession condition — and the rest is the seed author's signature image. The descriptive, contestable carvings are: the "shattered unity"; the "either believe one or the other"; the joint-*incoherence* thesis; and the signature arithmetic ("the 0 outside experience approximated as 1 and 0"). The canon does not carry the arithmetic forward. Where the seed says the two ultimates are jointly *incoherent*, the benchmark says only that they are jointly *unpossessable* — and that jointly-unpossessable reading is itself a treatise-side attribution of the benchmark's position.

VLS rejects **both** absolutism (*you do not possess truth or freedom*) **and** nihilism (*a semblance is not a lie; it is an appearance that participates in what it cannot exhaust*), without asserting the stronger incoherence thesis.

VLS *is the alethic axis of the treatise made into doctrine.* "A semblance that participates in what it cannot exhaust" is exactly the ceiling named for every accurate mapping. So VLS is not a special humility the framework wears; it is the framework recognizing that its own best claims — and physics's, and arithmetic's — all share the semblance-not-possession condition. The deflation of "forced" into "derived" *is* VLS applied to the framework's own status language.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, AC — the semblance-not-possession claim is the only point on VLS common to seed, benchmark, and this edition; contestable by contesting the authority.
- **A:** It is the framework's most direct statement of the universal no-possession condition, and it aspires to map it accurately — a semblance is not a lie but an appearance that participates in what it cannot exhaust.
- **E (carvings):** the shattered-unity/arithmetic image is Derived, seed-side CV — contestable by counter-instance, not carried by the authority.
- **A (carvings):** seed framing, low mapping-aspiration; the jointly-unpossessable reading aspires to map the relation of truth and freedom more accurately and more modestly than the incoherence thesis.

## Distinctions & cautions
- VLS is *not* the claim that absolute truth and absolute freedom are jointly *incoherent* — that is the seed's contestable carving. The carried position is that they are jointly *unpossessable*.
- The shattered-unity image and the 1/0 arithmetic are signature framing, not doctrine; the canon does not carry them forward.
- VLS is neither absolutism nor nihilism; a semblance is not a lie.
