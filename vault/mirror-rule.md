# The Mirror Rule

> **Reason is indispensable but not sovereign — the seed rule fixing the Logician's standing as the symmetry-center whose output should not bypass constraint or structure.**
> *Part IV · AC (the Logician's scored definition) · seed (rule) within canonical role*

## Definition
The seed's **Mirror Rule** fixes the Logician's standing: *reason is indispensable but not sovereign.* The Logician — differentiation, structure, coherence, consistency — is *the symmetry-center*; its output *should not bypass constraint or structure.*

## In depth
The [[logician|Logician]] is the faculty *that turns felt significance into structured intelligibility and checks the parts against each other and the whole against itself.* The Mirror Rule names how it must stand: indispensable, yes — without it felt significance never becomes structured intelligibility — but *not sovereign.* It is the symmetry-center, not the ruler. Its output may not bypass the constraint and structure it is bound to.

The rule is the guard against the Logician's standing danger: **sterile abstraction** — *coherence become domination over life, treating contradiction as impurity, ambiguity as weakness, emotion as contamination.* (Seed: *Logician capture risks sterile flattening.*) The Mirror Rule keeps reason in its place: a mirror that reflects and checks, not a sovereign that overrides what it reflects.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, AC — for the Logician's scored definition and danger (the Mirror Rule is the seed formulation of that AC standing); contestable by contesting the authority.
- **A:** The Logician's definition aspires to map a real, distinguishable faculty of meaning-processing and the characteristic pathology of its capture, and maps them accurately.

## Distinctions & cautions
"Reason indispensable" is not "reason sovereign" — the rule asserts the first and denies the second. The Logician is a faculty, *not a personality* (see function-not-personality). Treating coherence as the ruling value over life is exactly the sterile-abstraction failure the rule guards against.
