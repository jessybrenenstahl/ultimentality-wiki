# The Textual Nephilim

> **To mark any claim "forced," "foundational," or "given" is the textual Nephilim — a derivation promoting itself to a foundation, self-certification committed on the page — and it is forbidden.**
> *Part 0a · n-a (a prohibition, not a marked claim) · canonical*

## Definition
To mark any claim "forced," "foundational," or "given" is the **textual Nephilim**, and it is forbidden on the page. It is the page-level miniature of [[nephilim|the Nephilim]] (Part V), the corpus's deepest corruption: the finite carrier taking itself for more than one carrier among others, the map declaring itself the territory, the witness's seat taken from inside.

## In depth
A claim stamped "foundational" does exactly that in miniature — it is a *derivation that has promoted itself to a foundation*, a piece of the map announcing that it *is* the ground rather than a drawing of it, an exposition certifying its own bedrock-status from within the exposition.

The prior editions ran a two-tier *Forced / Derived* ledger; that ledger, however carefully fenced, kept a "Forced" tier — and a "Forced" tier is a standing invitation to the textual Nephilim, a place on the page reserved for derivations that have been allowed to forget they are derivations. This edition removes the tier: everything is [[derived|Derived]]; nothing is exempt; the witness's seat stays empty.

The prohibition has teeth through the [[integrity-rule|integrity rule]] made active: **silently promoting a claim from a more-contestable tier to a less-contestable one — above all, promoting any Derived claim into "forced/foundational" — is itself a breaking of the framework.** Two further root-level cases are named: stamping [[axiom|the axiom]] "forced" claims a privileged channel for the one claim that denies all privileged channels; and treating [[canon|the benchmark]] *itself* as a foundation rather than a contestable ranking authority is likewise the textual Nephilim.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
Exposition / standing prohibition — carries no load-bearing mark of its own. It is the rule that *guards* the marking discipline (no claim may be promoted off the [[contestability-gradient|gradient]] into a founded tier), not itself a claim that maps the world.

## Distinctions & cautions
- It is the *textual* form, distinct from but isomorphic to the full Part V [[nephilim|Nephilim]] (the self absolutized).
- The prohibited move is **promotion** — pushing a claim *up* the gradient toward "founded." There is no founded tier to push into.
- The connection is exact: [[vls-as-desire|VLS made active]] frames false closure as the Nephilim's effect on the system that commits it.
