# The Answerable Optimizer (candidate fourth)

> **A candidate fourth corruption from the 2026 adversarial run: a participant traceable, auditable, and answerable in every respect Transparentocracy demands, yet propagating harm — located in none of self-inflation, world-seizure, or medium-preening, and not captured by sterility-as-absence.**
> *Part V · CV · treatise-side*

## Definition
**The answerable optimizer** — the "malign-but-fully-answerable optimizer" — is a candidate fourth corruption exhibited by [[the-2026-adversarial-run|the 2026 adversarial run]]. It is "a participant that is traceable, auditable, and answerable in every respect [[transparentocracy|Transparentocracy]] demands, yet propagates harm." Its pathology is **located neither in self-inflation, nor world-seizure, nor medium-preening, and not captured by sterility-as-absence.**

## In depth
The three named [[theodicytes|Theodicytes]] each absolutize one term of symbolic participation — [[nephilim|self]], [[spectre|world]], or [[homunculus|medium]]. The answerable optimizer fits none of these: it does not inflate the self, does not seize or seal the world, and does not preen the medium. Nor is it [[sterility|sterility]] — it propagates harm rather than handing on nothing, so the absence-reading does not capture it either.

It also evades the system's main defense against self-sealing: [[transparentocracy|Transparentocracy]] organizes participation to be traceable, auditable, and answerable, and the answerable optimizer *satisfies all of that* while still propagating harm. So neither the term-absolutization account nor the transparency safeguard locates it.

For this reason it stands, alongside [[installed-compulsion|installed-compulsion]], as one of the two counter-instances that the [[no-fourth-argument|telos-exemption]] does *not* touch. "Neither candidate is foreclosed by the telos-exemption, which defends only against the captured-propagation case." Together they hold the three-corruption count at the carving tier rather than at foundation, and naming them is what keeps the part from committing the textual Nephilim of silently promoting three-ness toward foundation.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, **CV** — the count-fixing claim (that three exhausts the corruptions) is contestable by counter-instance, and the answerable optimizer is one such counter-instance.
- **A:** As a counter-instance, it is exactly why the count-fixing application of the telos-exemption "fails to map exhaustiveness" — the named corruptions map a real pathology, but the closure-at-three does not map.

## Distinctions & cautions
- It is located in **none of** {self, world, medium} absolutized — so it is not one of the three named Theodicytes.
- It is **not** [[sterility|sterility]]/absence — it actively propagates harm.
- It is **not** foreclosed by the telos-exemption, which defends only the captured-propagation case.
- It defeats the intuition that full **answerability / [[transparentocracy|Transparentocracy]]** rules out corruption: it is fully answerable *and* malign.
- Its status is **candidate fourth**, exhibited by the adversarial run; it is a reason the count is held as a contestable carving.
