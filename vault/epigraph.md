# The Epigraph

> **"Pinning Wittgenstein's Ladder to Heidegger's Floor using Christ's Nails and Nietzsche's Hammer." — four nouns and a verb mapping one-to-one onto the five SPLCW entities.**
> *Part IV · CV (carving) · treatise-side*

## Definition
The epigraph of the modern treatise is: *Pinning Wittgenstein's Ladder to Heidegger's Floor using Christ's Nails and Nietzsche's Hammer.* It and the entire reading-apparatus built on it are **treatise-side**. Its four nouns and one verb map one-to-one onto the five entities of [[splcw|SPLCW]] — a reading of an aphorism, not a scored claim.

## In depth
The one-to-one mapping:

- **Ladder = [[logician|Logician]]** — Wittgenstein's rungs of formal coherence, climbed and then thrown away.
- **Floor = [[warden|Warden]]** — Heidegger's ground of already-being-here, thrownness, the boundary you did not choose and operate against.
- **Nails = [[captive|Captive]]** — Christ's fastening that is also a wound: the faculties that hold whether or not you consent.
- **Hammer = [[poet|Poet]]** — Nietzsche's, read correctly: not only the hammer that destroys but the *tuning fork* struck against an idol to hear whether it rings hollow — symbolic transduction.
- **Pinning = [[sculptor|Sculptor]]** — not a noun but a gerund, an act; the one element happening rather than standing, the fifth that holds the ring open because an act in the present progressive is never finished.

The figure is **not a row but a ring** (see [[the-ring|The Ring]]): pinning needs the ladder, the ladder rests on the floor, the floor takes the nails, the nails are driven by the hammer, the hammer completes the pinning, and the pinning begins again.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, treatise-side CV (carving) — a reading of an aphorism, contestable by counter-instance or by declining the reading.
- **A:** The one-to-one mapping aspires to map the five faculties onto the figure illuminatingly; it is one apt construal, not a possession of the aphorism's meaning.

## Distinctions & cautions
- It is a **reading**, not a scored claim — contestable both by counter-instance and simply by declining the reading.
- The whole apparatus is **treatise-side**, not authority-canonical; do not import it as fixed doctrine.
- "Pinning" is deliberately a gerund: the Sculptor is the element *happening*, which is why the figure cannot close.
