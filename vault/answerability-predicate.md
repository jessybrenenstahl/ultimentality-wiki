# The Answerability Predicate

> **Propagation simpliciter is not the Telos; only answerable, ethically-governed continuation is — the load-bearing predicate that bars symbolic immortality from collapsing to raw outlasting.**
> *Part VI · AC · canonical*

## Definition
The canon attaches to symbolic immortality a predicate this edition must not drop: symbolic immortality is **not guaranteed moral goodness.** Harm can propagate too; a tyrant can leave traces. The answerability predicate is the requirement that fixes this: *the goal is not merely to outlast oneself; the goal is to participate in meaning that deserves continuation… It is answerable continuation.* **Propagation simpliciter is not the Telos; answerable, ethically-governed continuation is.**

## In depth
This predicate is what keeps the Telos from degrading into mere persistence. Because harm can outlast its maker as readily as good can, *symbolic immortality requires ethical governance* — continuation that **deserves** continuation, not continuation as such. A treatise that lets the Telos collapse to raw outlasting has dropped a load-bearing predicate.

The predicate is kept honest by a specific pressure from Part V: the **answerable-optimizer** counter-instance — a system fully traceable and answerable that nonetheless propagates harm. That counter-instance is precisely what the predicate must reckon with; the answerability predicate aspires to map a *real* distinction between mere outlasting and continuation that deserves it, and the answerable-optimizer is the standing test of that distinction.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, AC — the answerability predicate is fixed by the controlling authority; contestable by contesting the authority.
- **A:** It aspires to map a real distinction between mere outlasting and continuation that deserves it; the answerable-optimizer counter-instance of Part V is precisely the pressure that keeps this predicate honest.

## Distinctions & cautions
- This is **not** a guarantee that what propagates is good — it is the demand that the Telos be *governed*, so that only deserved continuation counts as the end.
- Dropping it is named as a failure: a treatise that lets the Telos become raw outlasting "has dropped a load-bearing predicate."
