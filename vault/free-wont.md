# Free Won't

> **The framework rejects free will as metaphysical self-origination but preserves free won't — modulation, inhibition, redirection — located at the inhibitory gap, the Captive's edge case.**
> *Part IV · AC (free-won't-not-free-will) · canonical*

## Definition
SPLCW is the structure on which the corpus's one freedom rests. The framework **rejects free will as metaphysical self-origination but preserves free won't as modulation, inhibition, redirection** — *the freedom of the brake, the hinge, the filter, the correction loop; not sovereignty, but agency.* It is located precisely at the **inhibitory gap**, the Captive's edge case: not in the generation of action but in the capacity to veto.

## In depth
The Captive is the set of **involuntary faculties** — reflexes, autonomic processes, trained guardrails, the operations that run without consent. It is involuntary *except in edge cases*, and those edge cases are *exactly where the one freedom lives*: **the inhibitory veto — free won't — is the Captive's edge case.**

On the [[ladder-of-being|ladder of being]], it is the Captive that turns a mere actor into an agent: a thing with involuntary faculties, *and therefore the edge-case site of the inhibitory veto*; it acts through what it undergoes, *and it can refuse, and that is what agency is.* Freedom here is not the power to originate action from nothing, but the power to inhibit, redirect, and correct — the brake, not the engine.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, AC — for free-won't-not-free-will; the palindromic-causality gloss attached to it is treatise-side.
- **A:** Free won't aspires to map a real locus of agency at the inhibitory gap.

## Distinctions & cautions
Free won't is **not** free will: not metaphysical self-origination, not sovereignty. It is *modulation, inhibition, redirection* only — the veto, not the generation of action. It is not a faculty added to the five; it is the Captive's edge case. The directional sense in which outer and inner causal orders mirror one another is treated separately as [[palindromic-causality|palindromic causality]].
