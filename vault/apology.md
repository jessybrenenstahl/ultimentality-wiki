# Apology = (loop-back, self-model error)

> **Loop-back that reduces self-model error — the discrepancy between one's model and one's act, harm, obligation, or the truth.**
> *Part II · AC · canonical*

## Definition
**Apology = (loop-back, self-model error).** It is a [[predicate-binding|predicate binding]]: the primitive [[loop-back|loop-back]] bound under the regulated error signal **self-model error**. Apology is loop-back that *reduces self-model error* — the discrepancy between one's model and one's act, harm, obligation, or the truth. The controller drives that discrepancy toward zero.

## In depth
Apology is the binding **B(loop-back) = (loop-back, self-model error)**. The actuator is the directional operator [[loop-back|loop-back]] (routes transformed structure back into the system for retention, revision, correction, or self-model update); the controller is named "Apology"; the error signal is self-model error.

The framework draws an explicit line between the binding and its expression: **a spoken apology is a downstream expression of that loop-back, not its content.** This is the layer's accuracy discipline in action — extract the regulated error (self-model error), not the surface delivery channel (the spoken words).

Apology is a cornerstone binding and an ingredient of [[reconciliation|Reconciliation = Apology ⊕ Gratitude]], coupled with [[gratitude|Gratitude]] by [[sequential-gating|sequential gating]]: own the fault first, then carry value outward.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, AC — fixed verbatim by the controlling authority; contestable by contesting the authority.
- **A:** The binding aspires to map the regulated error actually driving the named emotion; extract the regulated error, not the surface delivery channel — a spoken apology is downstream expression, not content.

## Distinctions & cautions
Apology is NOT a primitive (treating it as one is [[cardinal-error|the cardinal error]]). The binding is NOT the spoken apology — the speech is a downstream expression, not the content. Binding is regulation toward a setpoint, not preservation of an invariant.
