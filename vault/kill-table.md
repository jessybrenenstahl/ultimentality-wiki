# The Kill-Table

> **The standing record naming, for each load-bearing claim, what would refute it on each axis — the concrete enforcement of survivable refutation.**
> *Part VIII · structural · treatise-side (records canonical and run-side refutations)*

## Definition
The kill-table is the standing record the framework must hold: for each load-bearing claim, it names *what would refute it on each axis*. It is the operational form of the [[falsification-standard|falsification standard]] — acceptance as survivable refutation made into an explicit, maintained list.

## In depth
The clearest demonstration of the table is the complete list of what the [[the-2026-adversarial-run|2026 run]] refuted on the **epistemic axis** — each surviving intact on the **alethic axis** as an accurate mapping (*defeated as closure, kept as mapping*):

- **The four-primitive closure** — refuted by a candidate motion (a memory/temporal operation) the two-axis [[selection-axis|Selection]]/[[routing-axis|Routing]] carving does not obviously contain. *(Alethic: the decomposition still maps a transformation step accurately.)*
- **The two-Force closure** — refuted by a real in-domain cross-axis coupling (e.g. {Love, Gratitude}) the two same-axis Forces do not exhaust. *(Alethic: the two same-axis couplings still map the vertical and restorative relations accurately.)*
- **The three-corruption closure** — refuted by candidate fourth corruptions the telos-exemption does not foreclose: a *corruption by addition / installed-compulsion / sign-flip* (a presence in the wrong place that is not a single-term absolutization), and a *malign-but-fully-answerable optimizer* (harm-propagation located in none of self/world/medium and not reducible to sterility-as-absence). *(Alethic: the three named corruptions still map the framework's central pathologies accurately.)*
- **The five-role closure** — refuted by the absence of a palindrome/role-signature for the same memory/temporal operator. *(Alethic: the five roles still map five distinguishable faculties accurately.)*

All four count-closures fell on the epistemic axis; all four survive on the alethic axis. The three-corruption closure is on this list **explicitly**, demoted with the rest — listing it is required, because to omit it while reciting the others would be the silent-promotion move (the [[textual-nephilim|textual Nephilim]]) on the page. A further entry: the prior "strongest single piece of evidence" superlative was scored a **Nephilim-kill**, and dated precedence was demoted to testimony in consequence.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
The kill-table is the enforcement record, not a marked claim of its own. Each entry it holds points to a [[two-axis-ledger|ledger]] row and records *what kills it on each axis* — the epistemic refuter (counter-instance, declined frame, or contested authority) and the preserved alethic mapping.

## Distinctions & cautions
A "kill" on the epistemic axis is NOT a kill on the alethic axis — every count-closure fell as a closure while its mapping survived. The table records refutation conditions, not verdicts of falsity. The run-side counter-instances are held *under* the benchmark's [[open-proof-burden|open proof-burden]], never as an authority above it.
