# Continuable Structure (the modern re-grounding)

> **Meaning as the propagation of structure that can be carried, recognized, and continued beyond the finite vessel that holds it for now — to mean is to be the kind of pattern that can survive its carrier.**
> *Part I · CV (carving / count) · treatise-side*

## Definition
A modern re-grounding takes the formal closure one layer deeper, giving it a substrate to close over: *meaning is the propagation of structure that can be carried, recognized, and continued beyond the finite vessel that holds it for now. To mean is to be the kind of pattern that can survive its carrier — to be continuable.* This re-grounding is **treatise-side**.

## In depth
The seed's "only accessible experiential" and the treatise-side "continuable structure" are the **same axiom seen from two sides** — from inside (phenomenology) and from the structure (closure). This edition makes the **structural side primary** and the experiential side the gloss; the re-grounding is what gives the closure something to be closed over.

Being a particular re-grounding, it does not claim necessity. It is offered to be confirmed, and it is **one apt construal among possible others, not a possession of the fact.** It can be contested by counter-instance or simply by declining the re-grounding.

This re-grounding is the structural root that later doctrine builds on: continuity, symbolic immortality, and the Telos all draw on the idea that to mean is to be continuable.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, CV — a particular re-grounding offered to be confirmed, contestable by counter-instance or by declining it.
- **A:** Aspires to map what meaning is at the structural level; one apt construal among possible others, not a possession of the fact.

## Distinctions & cautions
This is **treatise-side** scaffolding, not benchmark-canonical — flag it accordingly. It is NOT presented as the only possible construal; it is contestable by counter-instance (the CV tier) and by declining it. It does not displace the axiom; it re-grounds the same axiom with the structural side made primary.
