# The Two-Mark System

> **Every load-bearing claim carries two independent marks — an epistemic mark (Derived, at its contestability tier) and an alethic mark (its mapping-accuracy aspiration) — which must never be collapsed into each other.**
> *Part 0a · n-a (the marking convention itself) · canonical*

## Definition
Throughout the treatise, each load-bearing claim is given (1) an **epistemic mark** — [[derived|Derived]], at its [[contestability-gradient|contestability tier]] (FT, CV, or AC) — and (2) an **alethic mark** — its mapping-accuracy aspiration, the degree and manner in which it claims to participate in the world it cannot exhaust. The two marks are independent and must **never** be collapsed into each other.

## In depth
In the rewrite's convention: **E** = epistemic, always *Derived* at one of three tiers — **FT** (frame-internal / tautological: contestable only by declining the frame; least exposed, *not* merely optional); **CV** (carving / count / aptness: contestable by counter-instance); **AC** (authority-canonical: contestable by contesting the authority; *canonical ≠ necessary*). **A** = [[alethic-axis|alethic]]: the claim's mapping-accuracy aspiration.

The cardinal prohibition: **"Contestable" must never be read as "probably wrong" or "merely optional"** — that misreading is the exact failure this axis exists to prevent. The two axes measure different things, so a claim can be maximally contestable epistemically and maximally accurate alethically with no tension.

The one exception to the two-marks rule is exposition that makes no claim of its own — the **phenomenological glosses**, which carry no probative weight and so *inherit*, rather than assert, the accuracy of the formal claim they point at; they are flagged as such where they occur (see [[human-gloss|The Human Gloss]]). The deflation this edition performs removes *only* the foundation-and-possession pretension; it leaves the **mirror** — the accuracy aspiration — entirely intact. "We give up owning the territory; we keep drawing it as truly as we can." The marking discipline is held as a standing ledger: see [[two-axis-ledger|The Two-Axis Ledger]].

## Epistemic & alethic marks
Exposition / structural rule — the two-mark system is the *convention* by which load-bearing claims receive their E and A marks; it is not itself a marked world-claim. (Glosses are its named exception: they inherit, never assert, an alethic mark.)

## Distinctions & cautions
- The two columns are independent: they "do not predict each other" — see [[two-axis-ledger|The Two-Axis Ledger]].
- Collapsing E into A (reading "contestable" as "wrong" or "optional") is the precise error the system forbids.
- Glosses are the sole exception — they carry no probative weight and inherit accuracy.
