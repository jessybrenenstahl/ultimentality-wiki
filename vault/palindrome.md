# The Palindrome

> **The five SPLCW entities read as symmetric about the involuntary Captive: Warden — Logician — Captive — Poet — Sculptor, and back.**
> *Part IV · symmetry-about-the-Captive is AC (authority-canonical); the specific left-to-right order is treatise-side CV (carving) · mixed*

## Definition
This edition reads the five entities as a palindrome about the involuntary core: *[[warden|Warden]] — [[logician|Logician]] — [[captive|Captive]] — [[poet|Poet]] — [[sculptor|Sculptor]], and back.* The Warden (boundary-in) mirrors the Sculptor (mutation-out) across the self's interface; the Logician (internal coherence) mirrors the Poet (external answerability) across the symbolic membrane; the [[captive|Captive]] sits at the center, the involuntary hinge on which the whole turns.

## In depth
The seed grounds the palindromic intuition — SPLCW as *a symbolic, palindromic interpretation of how causality precludes free will* — though the seed runs the palindrome over the *processing* order (W-C-L-P-S), in which the Captive comes **second, not central.**

**Honesty note (the writer's, not the corpus's).** *That the structure is symmetric about the involuntary Captive is the canonical claim*, but the specific left-to-right ordering printed here — and in particular which of Logician and Poet sits on which side — is **the writer's reconstruction, offered to be confirmed, not asserted as settled.** If the corpus fixes a canonical linear order, that order governs; note the tension with the canonical processing order, in which the Captive is second.

The recursive [[operator-chain|operator-chain]] order (Warden → Captive → Logician → Poet → Sculptor) is stated separately and is **not touched** by this reconstruction.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** The symmetry-about-the-Captive is Derived, AC (authority-canonical); the specific left-to-right order is Derived, treatise-side reconstruction, CV (carving), contestable by counter-instance.
- **A:** The symmetry aspires to map a real structural fact; the specific ordering is a candidate mapping offered to be confirmed, not a possession.

## Distinctions & cautions
- **Split provenance:** the symmetry-about-the-Captive is canonical; the exact left-to-right order is treatise-side and explicitly offered to be confirmed.
- **Two orderings coexist:** the palindrome's mirror order (W-L-C-P-S) is distinct from the seed's *processing* order (W-C-L-P-S, Captive second) and from the [[operator-chain|operator chain]] (W-C-L-P-S as canonical cycle). There is a flagged tension over where the Captive sits.
- If the corpus fixes a canonical linear order, **that order governs** over the writer's reconstruction.
