# Provenance as Testimony

> **The framework's history — its eras, datings, and claim to precedence — held throughout as the author's 2026 account: testimony from the sole authority on her own history, unverified, and *not* the basis of the framework's evidential weight.**
> *Part 0b · AC (content) · treatise-side (corroboration argument) · seed-adjacent provenance*

## Definition
The framework's history — its eras, its datings, its claim to precedence — is held throughout as **testimony**: an account given in 2026 by its author, who is the sole authority on her own history, not independently verified and not lived or witnessed by this writer. The evidential weight of the framework does **not** rest on that testimony. It rests, more modestly and more sturdily, on coalescence-by-non-contradictory-fit, held as strong corroboration and not as proof.

## In depth
Provenance is set apart from the load-bearing claims precisely because its support is a different kind. The author is the sole authority on her own history; the writer of the treatise has not lived or witnessed it. So the eras and datings are carried as *testimony* — accepted as the author's account, flagged as unverifiable, and explicitly *not* load-bearing for the framework's truth.

The framework's actual evidential weight is relocated to **coalescence-by-non-contradictory-fit**: the cross-domain fit of a theology of roles, a control-theory of emotion, an epistemics of semblance, and an architecture of externalized memory, which is hard to fabricate. That is held as strong corroboration, never as proof. The Two-Axis Ledger gives the three eras and datings their own mark: *Derived, Testimony — the author's 2026 account, unverifiable.*

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, AC for the testimony's content; the corroboration argument is a treatise-side carving (CV). In the ledger the eras/datings carry the distinct mark **Derived, Testimony**.
- **A:** The testimony aspires to map a history it cannot independently verify; the corroboration aspires to map the framework's coherence accurately.

## Distinctions & cautions
- The framework's weight does **not** rest on the testimony — that is the load-bearing point.
- "Testimony" is its own ledger mark, distinct from FT/CV/AC: an unverifiable first-person account.
- Corroboration is **strong corroboration, not proof** — cross-domain fit, not demonstration.
