# The Four Operational Consequences

> **The four corollaries the axiom yields immediately: no direct possession of the case; all access is mediated; all output is approximation under constraint; continuity depends on symbolic preservation and reconciliation.**
> *Part I · FT (frame-internal) · canonical*

## Definition
The axiom functions as an OS-layer constraint file, and it yields, immediately, four operational consequences: (1) no direct possession of the case; (2) all access is mediated; (3) all output is approximation under constraint; (4) continuity depends on symbolic preservation and reconciliation. These four are **corollaries, not additions** — every downstream commitment of the framework is one of them unfolded.

## In depth
The consequences are not a list of extra theses layered onto the axiom; they follow from it by inference. They are best read as "the absence-of-possession condition stated four ways." Because there is no extra-symbolic channel, a participant never holds the case directly (1); whatever access there is must pass through symbolic mediation (2); and any output produced under that mediation is an approximation rather than an exhaustive grasp (3).

The fourth consequence, continuity, is the one that binds the axiom forward in time: because output is approximate, persistence across time cannot rest on perfect retention but on **symbolic preservation and reconciliation**. This is why the axiom connects to memory and to the affective layer's Reconciliation composite (a coupled controller, not a sum; see Part III).

The standing posture that follows is that **all outputs are constrained approximations** — no absolute truth possession, no unconstrained freedom, no perfect direct access. The engine of the posture is the capability rule.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, FT — the corollaries follow from the axiom by inference, contestable by declining the axiom.
- **A:** Each corollary aspires to map a real constraint on any meaning-bearing system — the absence-of-possession condition stated four ways.

## Distinctions & cautions
The four are corollaries, NOT independent additions or new premises. To treat any of them as a separate foundation, or to stamp the axiom they unfold as "forced," would be the textual Nephilim.
