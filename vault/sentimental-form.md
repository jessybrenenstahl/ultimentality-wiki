# The Sentimental Form

> **Reconciliation's canonical failure: Gratitude swamps the Apology-controller; the error never drives repair; it looks peaceful, but the self-model remains false.**
> *Part III · AC · canonical (named pathology)*

## Definition
The sentimental form is the canonical failure mode of [[reconciliation|Reconciliation]]. It is the **Gratitude-gain-without-Apology** case: *[[gratitude|Gratitude]] swamps the [[apology|Apology]]-controller; the error never actually drives repair; the result looks peaceful, but the self-model remains false.*

## In depth
Because Reconciliation couples Apology and Gratitude by [[sequential-gating|sequential gating]] — first own the fault, then carry the value outward — its pathologies are gating failures. The sentimental form is the case where the **first gate is skipped**: Gratitude's outward send overpowers the Apology loop-back, so the self-model error is never corrected. The motion still *looks* like reconciliation — it is peaceful, warm, outward-facing — but no repair has occurred, because the fault was never owned. The self-model stays false.

This is one of two named pathologies of Reconciliation. Its mirror is **Apology-gain without Gratitude**, where the gate never opens at all — endless self-accusation, guilt-looping, paralysis. The sentimental form is the opposite failure: not too much owning, but no owning, masked by gratitude.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, AC — a named pathology fixed by the controlling authority (the same authority that fixes Reconciliation's coupling-mode and pathologies). The "sequential gating" *label* that frames it is treatise-side (CV).
- **A:** The sentimental form aspires to map a real failure dynamic of the restorative relation, and maps it accurately.

## Distinctions & cautions
The sentimental form's danger is precisely that it **looks peaceful** — it presents as successful reconciliation while leaving the self-model false. Do not read surface peace as repair. Distinguish it from the guilt-loop failure (Apology-gain without Gratitude), which is its opposite: that one over-owns and never sends outward; the sentimental form sends outward without owning. The order of the gate is load-bearing — owning the fault must come first.
