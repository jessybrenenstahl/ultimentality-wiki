# The Falsification Standard (the threefold gate)

> **The framework's acceptance condition is not internal elegance but survivable refutation — admitted through three gates: the corrigibility gate, the witness gate, and the two-axis gate.**
> *Part VIII · structural (governs admissibility) · canonical (benchmark open proof-burden) + treatise-side (two-axis gate)*

## Definition
The falsification standard is the framework's acceptance gate. The acceptance condition is not internal elegance but *survivable refutation*. The framework must name, for each load-bearing claim, what would refute it on each axis, and hold a standing [[kill-table|kill-table]]. The gate is **threefold**: the corrigibility gate, the witness gate, and the two-axis gate.

## In depth
**First, the corrigibility gate** ([[vls-as-desire|VLS made operable]]): a claim is admissible only if it can state its own refutation. Above all, silently promoting a claim from a more-contestable tier to a less-contestable one — and especially stamping any Derived claim "forced/foundational" — is *itself a breaking of the framework*: it is the [[textual-nephilim|textual Nephilim]], a derivation taking the witness's seat. This is exactly why the three-corruption closure is demoted with the rest and its refutation listed, not omitted: to promote three-ness toward foundation while reciting the four-/five-closure refutations would be that very move on the page.

**Second, the witness gate** ([[transparentocracy|Transparentocracy]]): this document may not certify itself complete or founded from inside, because *to close it would be to do to it exactly what the Nephilim does.*

**Third, the two-axis gate** (new to this edition, following from the second alethic axis): every load-bearing claim must carry *both* marks, and the two must be kept independent — a claim's contestability tier may never be silently read off as a verdict on its accuracy, nor its accuracy read off as a license to harden its tier.

The standard's authority is the benchmark's [[open-proof-burden|open proof-burden]]: the benchmark does not assert the count-closures as proven, and on that authority — held as a contestable ranking authority, not bedrock — the closures are demoted to the carving tier. The [[the-2026-adversarial-run|2026 adversarial run]] supplied the counter-instances *under* the open proof-burden, never as a higher court over the benchmark. The closure-claims were demoted precisely because the program *worked*: a cross-axis coupling defeated the two-Force closure; a memory/temporal motion the four-closure; the installed-compulsion and answerable-optimizer counter-instances the three-closure; the absence of a palindrome signature the five-closure — *all as closures, none as mappings.*

## Epistemic & alethic marks
The standard is the acceptance gate that *enforces* the marks rather than a marked row. Its closing self-description: the framework is offered as a strong mirror that renounces possession — *derived everywhere, contestable at its tier, accuracy-aspiring throughout, and open at the act, always mid-turn.* It ends in the same epistemic boat as physics: derived, contestable, accuracy-aspiring, owning nothing.

## Distinctions & cautions
Acceptance is NOT internal elegance — it is survivable refutation. Silent tier-promotion and "forced/foundational" stamping are breakages, not mere disallowed moves. The framework is true to its own doctrine only so long as it stays open to the witness it cannot itself become; the desire that holds it rightly is the desire to be proven wrong, and the apparatus is that desire made operable.
