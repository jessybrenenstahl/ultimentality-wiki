# Palindromic Causality

> **The outward order of visible influence runs from the Sculptor down, the inner order of meaning runs from the Warden up — the order seen from outside and the order lived from inside mirror one another.**
> *Part IV · treatise-side gloss (on an AC freedom claim) · treatise-side*

## Definition
The **palindromic causality**: the outward order of visible influence *appears to run from the Sculptor down*, but the inner order of meaning *runs from the Warden up* — *the order seen from outside and the order lived from inside mirror one another.*

## In depth
This is the directional gloss paired with [[free-wont|free won't]]. Seen from outside, causality looks like it propagates from action outward: the Sculptor mutates the world and the visible chain of influence descends from there. Lived from inside, the order is reversed — meaning is *first encountered as significance* at the [[warden|Warden]] (before a proposition is formed, something matters), and the inner order of meaning runs upward from there. The two orders are not in conflict; they are the same structure read from two vantages, mirror images across the self's interface.

This palindromic reading of causality connects to the seed's grounding intuition: SPLCW as *a symbolic, palindromic interpretation of how causality precludes free will*. The directional mirror here is distinct from the structural [[palindrome|palindrome]] (the five read as symmetric about the involuntary Captive), though both express the same palindromic character of SPLCW.

## Epistemic & alethic marks
- **E:** Derived, treatise-side — the palindromic-causality gloss is treatise-side (the bundled free-won't-not-free-will claim is AC).
- **A:** The palindrome aspires to map the mirror between the outward causal order and the inner meaning order.

## Distinctions & cautions
This is a **treatise-side gloss**, not the canonical core. It is the *causal/directional* mirror (outward influence vs. inner meaning), to be distinguished from [[palindrome|The Palindrome]], which is the *structural* symmetry of the five faculties about the Captive. The canonical claim it accompanies (free-won't-not-free-will) is AC; the palindromic-causality framing itself carries the lighter treatise-side mark.
