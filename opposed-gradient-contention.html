    Opposed-Gradient Contention

    Submission's coupling-mode: Love and Fear do not cancel but contend along opposed gradients, and out of that contention Submission emerges. Part III · AC (for the coupling-mode itself) / CV (for the exact label) · canonical coupling-mode, treatise-side label

    Definition

    Opposed-gradient contention is the specific regulatory relation by which Love and Fear couple into Submission. Love pulls toward, reducing relational gap; Fear pushes away, reducing boundary violation. Coupled, the two controllers do not cancel — they contend along opposed gradients, and out of that contention the Force of Submission emerges.

    In depth

    The two controllers share a transition while driving opposed but co-required error signals toward tolerable bounds. The opposition is structural: both are Selection-axis controllers acting on the same domain-side of the transformation step, so their gradients point against each other. That is why this coupling contends rather than gates — the asymmetry with Reconciliation's sequential gating is produced by which primitives are coupled, not stipulated.

    Because the contention is between opposed gradients rather than an additive sum, Submission's pathologies are gain-imbalances of the contention itself: Fear-gain dominance (servility, panic, coerced obedience) and unbounded Love-gain (self-erasure, dependency, boundary collapse), with the trapped form as the canonical lock-up where both gradients hold the actuator shut. The healthy equilibrium is yielding-to without self-erasure and without flight.

    Epistemic & alethic marks

    • E: Derived, AC for the coupling-mode (the fact that Submission couples by opposed contention is canonical); the exact phrase "opposed-gradient contention" is a treatise-side carving (CV), contestable by counter-instance.
    • A: The contention reading aspires to map how the vertical relation actually behaves — co-required opposed error signals and their imbalance pathologies — and maps it accurately where static conjunction does not.

    Distinctions & cautions

    Contention is not cancellation: the opposed gradients do not net to zero, they co-require each other. It is also not addition. Do not confuse it with sequential gating — gating is a temporal order (inward then outward), contention is simultaneous opposition. The label is the writer's scaffolding; the coupling-mode it names is fixed by the authority.
