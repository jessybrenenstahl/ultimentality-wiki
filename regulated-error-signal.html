    The Regulated Error Signal

    eₚ — the discrepancy a controller drives toward zero; the component of a predicate binding that names what the bound emotion is actually regulating. Part II · AC · canonical

    Definition

    In the binding B(p) = (p, eₚ), the term eₚ is a regulated error signal driven toward zero. It is the discrepancy the controller reduces. Where the primitive p is the actuator and the predicate names the controller, the error signal names the discrepancy the controller reduces. It is the heart of a binding: change the error signal and you change the emotion.

    In depth

    The error signal is what makes a binding a regulation toward a setpoint rather than the preservation of an invariant. The governing correction states it directly: binding is regulation toward a setpoint … the error signal names the discrepancy the controller reduces. For the four cornerstone bindings the error signal is named verbatim:

    • relational gap — the discrepancy between current separation and desired bond or continuity of coupling (Love).
    • boundary violation — the discrepancy between current exposure and acceptable protection against threat (Fear).
    • self-model error — the discrepancy between one's model and one's act, harm, obligation, or the truth (Apology).
    • undischarged received value — the discrepancy between value received and value carried forward (Gratitude).

    The accuracy discipline of the whole layer is to extract the regulated error, not the surface delivery channel — e.g. a spoken apology is a downstream expression of the loop-back, not its content. The edition's withdrawal of the invented "integrity" axis restored Fear's error signal to boundary violation.

    Epistemic & alethic marks

    • E: Derived, AC — the binding model that defines eₚ is fixed and capped by the controlling authority; contestable by contesting the authority.
    • A: Each named error signal aspires to map the regulated error actually driving the emotion; extracting the regulated error rather than the surface channel is the accuracy discipline that keeps the mapping honest.

    Distinctions & cautions

    The error signal is NOT a conserved invariant — treating the binding as invariant-preservation is a capped error. It is NOT the surface expression (the spoken word, the gesture). Fear's signal is boundary violation, not the withdrawn "integrity."
