    Loop-back

    Routes transformed structure back into the system for retention, revision, correction, or self-model update. Part II · AC · canonical

    Definition

    Loop-back is one of the four directional primitives. Its operational semantics, authority-canonical: it routes transformed structure back into the system for retention, revision, correction, or self-model update. It sits on the Routing axis — the codomain-side, where the transformed result is routed.

    In depth

    Loop-back is a bare directional operator, not a feeling. With its pair Propagation, it forms the Routing axis: the codomain-side of a recursive transformation step, governing where the transformed result is routed. Loop-back is the inward routing — back into the system itself.

    The seed names this pair inward / outward (symbols ↺ ↻); the rename to loop-back and propagation is exact and structure-preserving — terminology, not architecture. It is not an emotion, mood, virtue, image, or personality style; it is unexperienced structure, and the felt quality of the same name is gloss with no probative weight. Loop-back is load-bearing in the next rung: it is the actuator in Apology = (loop-back, self-model error), the loop-back that reduces the discrepancy between one's model and one's act, harm, obligation, or the truth. The conceptual derivative inwardness is a human-readable noun abstracted from the bound primitive — not itself primitive.

    Epistemic & alethic marks

    • E: Derived, AC — fixed by the controlling authority; contestable by contesting the authority.
    • A: Aspires to map an elementary directional move of any transformation step (route the result back in); the operator is bare and unexperienced, and that is the accurate claim.

    Distinctions & cautions

    Loop-back is NOT Apology. Apology is a predicate binding over loop-back; the predicate does not define the primitive. Reading loop-back as the emotion, or as a metaphor, inverts the layers — the cardinal error. Routing does not collapse into Selection: the four combinations are behaviorally distinct.
